What does an employer pay in German social security?

German employer social security adds approximately 20.7 percent on top of gross salary in 2026, split across five branches: pension 9.3%, health 7.3% plus half the average 2.9% Zusatzbeitrag, unemployment 1.3%, long-term care 1.8%, and accident insurance roughly 1.14%. Contributions stop once gross pay clears the relevant Beitragsbemessungsgrenze.

Two ceilings move every year through the Sozialversicherungsrechengrößen-Verordnung. For 2026: €5,812.50 / month (€69,750 / year) for health and long-term care, and €8,450 / month (€101,400 / year) for pension and unemployment. Once a salary clears the ceiling, employer contributions to that branch stop on the excess.

Above €77,400 / year (€6,450 / month) the employee can opt out of statutory health insurance into private health (PKV). That's the Versicherungspflichtgrenze, reset annually under the same regulation.

What changes in 2026 vs 2025

Headline contribution rates didn't move. The thresholds did: BBG health/care rose 5.4 percent, BBG pension/unemployment rose 5 percent, and the PKV opt-out threshold rose 4.9 percent. For a Berlin-based developer on a €90,000 base, that's roughly €420 / year in additional employer pension contributions that wouldn't have been due in 2025.

What does an employee pay in German social security?

German employee social security is the mirror of the employer split: 9.3% pension, 7.3% health (plus half of the 2.9% Zusatzbeitrag), 1.3% unemployment, 3.6% long-term care (an extra 0.6 percent if the employee is childless and over 23). Total roughly 20.7 percent of gross, deducted monthly via PAYE alongside Lohnsteuer.

Contributions are calculated on a monthly basis, not annual. A €30,000 one-off bonus paid in March still hits the monthly Pflegeversicherung cap, so the effective contribution on the bonus is usually lower than a salary-equivalent spread across the year.

What are the German income tax bands in 2026?

German income tax (Einkommensteuer) uses a continuous progressive formula in four zones for 2026: 0 percent up to €12,348 (Grundfreibetrag), 14 percent rising smoothly to 42 percent by €68,480, a flat 42 percent up to €277,825, then 45 percent above (the Reichensteuer). Rates didn't change in 2026; the Grundfreibetrag rose €252.

Germany doesn't bracket like the United Kingdom or the United States. Between €12,348 and €68,480 the marginal rate climbs as a continuous mathematical function defined in § 32a EStG, so every additional euro you earn raises the rate marginally and net pay scales smoothly rather than stepping at thresholds. Teamed's payroll engine applies the formula directly, no rounding to bracket boundaries.

Solidaritätszuschlag and Kirchensteuer

Two surcharges sit on top of Einkommensteuer. Solidaritätszuschlag is 5.5 percent of income tax, but in 2026 only payable if the single-filer income tax bill exceeds roughly €20,350 (joint: €40,700). Most Teamed-employed residents fall under the threshold and pay €0. Kirchensteuer is 8 percent of income tax in Bayern and Baden-Württemberg, 9 percent in every other state, and only applies to registered members of the recognised churches (Catholic, Protestant, Jewish communities). An employee removes themselves by formal Kirchenaustritt at the local Standesamt; Teamed's payroll picks up the change from the next month's ELSTAM data.

How does Lohnsteuer withholding work through Steuerklassen?

Lohnsteuer is the monthly withholding of income tax through PAYE. The amount withheld depends on the employee's Steuerklasse (tax class I through VI), which determines monthly cash-flow timing, not annual liability. Annual liability is identical regardless of class; the class only changes when the tax is paid.

Married couples can switch class combinations once per calendar year via ELSTER or paper application to the local Finanzamt. The choice doesn't change what you owe by year-end. It changes whether you owe more or less per pay slip and whether you'll be refunded or asked for additional tax when the annual return is filed.

ELSTAM (Elektronische Lohnsteuerabzugsmerkmale) is the federal database that holds every employee's tax class, allowances, and church-membership status. Teamed pulls ELSTAM data monthly; any change the employee files (marriage, child, Kirchenaustritt) flows through to the next payroll cycle automatically.

When are Lohnsteueranmeldung and Lohnsteuerbescheinigung due?

Two recurring filings to the Finanzamt: Lohnsteueranmeldung (monthly payroll tax return) is due by the 10th of the following month for any employer whose annual wage tax exceeds €5,000, and the annual Lohnsteuerbescheinigung (employee certificate of wages) must reach the employee by 28 February of the following year.

01 ELSTER Filing Cadence

Lohnsteueranmeldung files electronically through ELSTER. Frequency tracks annual wage tax: monthly above €5,000, quarterly between €1,080 and €5,000, annually below €1,080. Lohnsteuerbescheinigung is the annual employee certificate, due 28 February of the following year.

What is the German minimum wage in 2026?

Germany's statutory minimum wage (gesetzlicher Mindestlohn) is €13.90 per hour from 1 January 2026, up from €12.82 in 2025. A further increase to €14.60 per hour is scheduled for 1 January 2027, set by the Mindestlohnkommission.

The minimum wage applies to almost every employee in Germany, including mini-jobbers, working students, and most interns. Exempt categories are narrow: under-18s without a completed Berufsausbildung, certain mandatory internships, and people in long-term unemployment for the first six months of a new role.

The 2026 minimum-wage move also bumped the mini-job ceiling to €603 / month (it's pegged at the minimum wage × 130 hours / month / 12 × ratio). A mini-jobber working at minimum wage maxes out at around 43 hours / month before crossing into Midijob territory and full social-security liability.

Sector-specific minima set by allgemeinverbindlich tariff agreements override the statutory floor where they apply (construction, building cleaning, security, electrical trades, others). Teamed's payroll picks the higher of the two when a tariff applies to the role.
