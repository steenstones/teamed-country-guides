### Sources of factual conflict (flag in HIGH_CONCERN)

- Most aggregator sites cite "20-factor test" as the IRS's current test. The IRS officially replaced the 20-factor test with the three-category framework in 1996, formalised in Pub 15-A. Lead with the three-category framework and reference the 20-factor antecedent.
- DOL Final Rule (March 2024) restored the multi-factor economic reality test. The Trump-era 2021 rule that simplified to two core factors is superseded. Use the 2024 rule.
- ABC test state count varies by source (23 to 33). Use "around 30" and qualify "for at least one purpose" — some states use it only for unemployment, not income tax.

KERNAL voice rules apply verbatim: no forbidden words, no em dashes, sentence case headings, contractions, second person, specific numbers, data before narrative.

Topic-specific framing (lifted from UK IR35 logic, adapted for US):
- Contractor classification is a tax-status question, not a contractual one
- The contract terms matter; the actual working arrangement matters more
- EOR doesn't cure misclassification. Putting an at-risk contractor through an EOR can be evidence the worker was already an employee
- When the engagement was always going to be employment, EOR is one of three legitimate paths
- Don't buy synthetic contractor status through clever contracting. The IRS looks through it
