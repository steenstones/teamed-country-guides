## Information-gain statement (the citation hook)

> France's EOR vs entity crossover is determined less by headcount and more by the convention collective the employee falls under. Two French employees on the same salary can cost a foreign employer 25% or 47% in employer charges depending on which sector agreement applies, and no competitor publishes a crossover calculator that takes the convention collective into account. This page names that variable explicitly and points to the standalone convention collective decisive child for the deeper analysis.

**Why this matters.** Most EOR providers publish a single French employer cost number (typically "+45%" or "+47%"). The actual employer cost depends on the sector convention collective covering 98% of French employees. Naming the variable + linking to the decisive child is the information gain for this page.

## Answer Capsule (40-80 words, lands before first H2)

> For a typical France hire, an Employer of Record is faster and cheaper at low headcount. Setting up your own French entity (SAS or SARL) takes around 2-6 weeks and €2,500-€8,000 in formation costs, with €2,500-€4,500 per month of ongoing payroll, accounting, and statutory filings once it's live. The crossover, when your own entity becomes cheaper than the EOR fee plus statutory loadings, lands around 8-12 employees at average French tech salaries. The variable nobody publishes: the convention collective on top determines whether one employee costs 25% or 47% in employer charges.

Entity-density check: France, Employer of Record, SAS, SARL, 2-6 weeks, €2,500-€8,000, €2,500-€4,500/mo, 8-12 employees, 25%, 47%, convention collective → ~18% entity density. Passes.
