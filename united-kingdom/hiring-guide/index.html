If your candidate accepted on Friday and you start onboarding on Monday, you have already lost the week. The good ones get a second offer in that gap.

A non-sponsored UK hire, British, Irish, or settled-status, can be offer-letter to first payslip in 48 hours. A Skilled Worker visa hire is 8 to 10 weeks from sponsorship application to start date. This page covers the five-step cadence, the right-to-work check, the Section 1 statement, and where the Skilled Worker route adds eight weeks you cannot compress.

What does the end-to-end UK hire process look like?

From accepted offer to first payslip, every UK hire goes through the same five steps: offer letter, right-to-work check, Section 1 statement, onboarding admin, first payday.

Emma is a Manchester product designer with a UK passport. She accepts your offer at 4pm on Wednesday. By Friday afternoon she has signed her contract, cleared her right-to-work check, has a Teamed payroll record, and is on the qualifying pension scheme. Her first payslip lands at the end of the month.

Hiroshi is a developer in Tokyo you want for a London role. He needs a Skilled Worker visa. Same five steps, plus the sponsorship pipeline: certificate of sponsorship, visa application, biometrics, decision, travel. Ten weeks from offer accept to start date is a realistic plan. The offer letter is not the contract, but it is the commitment

The offer letter is the document the candidate says yes to. The Section 1 statement is the document a tribunal reads. Both have to line up.

A standard UK offer letter names the role, line manager, start date, salary, working pattern, location, notice period, probation period, the benefits summary, and any conditions: right-to-work, references, medical clearance.

Emma’s offer letter quotes a salary band, a 6-month probation, and the standard Teamed benefits stack. It does not promise a bonus payout or a future promotion. That is deliberate.

Three traps that turn a clean offer into a tribunal claim:

  • Overstating discretionary benefits. "Our bonus pays out at 100 percent of target" can become a contractual entitlement if the candidate relies on it. Discretion needs to be visible in the wording.
  • Inconsistent dates. Offer letter says one start date, the Section 1 statement says another. Tribunal-bait.
  • Promising things outside your authority. A line like "we will review for promotion at 6 months" without manager sign-off creates a constructive-dismissal angle if you do not honour it.

The standard UK offer letter template at Teamed covers every statutory point without overcommitting on the commercial. Clients edit the salary, the bonus framing, and the restrictive-covenant block. The legal employer name and statutory clauses stay locked.

The right-to-work check is the day-zero gate

Every UK employee needs a right-to-work check, or RTW, before they start. Skip it and the civil penalty starts at £45,000 per employee for a first offence, £60,000 for a repeat. There is no grace period.

Emma generates a share code on gov.uk in two minutes. The employer enters the code and her date of birth on the Home Office online checking service. The system returns a statutory excuse certificate that lands in her personnel file the same hour. Done.

For Hiroshi the order flips. The Skilled Worker visa is the right to work. The check still happens, but only after the visa is in his passport, eight weeks into the timeline.

Time-limited right to work needs a calendar entry on day one

Pre-settled status and time-limited visas expire. The follow-up check has to happen before the expiry date, or the statutory excuse falls away from the day after. The fix is calendar discipline. The Teamed UK People Ops team books the follow-up the same day the original check is filed, and prompts the employee with notice ahead of the deadline.

The Section 1 statement is the contract

The Section 1 statement is the written particulars of employment every UK worker has to receive on or before day one of work. This is the legally-binding contract. The offer letter is not.

Miss the day-one deadline and the worker can take you to a tribunal for an award of 2 to 4 weeks’ pay. The fix is cheap: the statement signs at the same time as the offer letter, well before day one.

The obligation extended to workers, not just employees, in April 2020. Casual workers, zero-hour workers, anyone in a working relationship with the legal employer gets the statement.

What happens in the first week of onboarding?

Days 1 to 7 carry the admin load: PAYE registration, pension auto-enrolment, benefits setup, the P45 or new-starter checklist, bank details, equipment, system access, manager intro, and a 30-60-90 day plan.

On Emma’s first day she submits her P45 from her previous role. Teamed picks up her existing tax code from HMRC, enrols her on the qualifying pension, configures private medical, and pays her by BACS at month-end. Her manager owns equipment, access, and her first-fortnight plan.

The Skilled Worker route adds eight weeks you cannot compress

Hiroshi’s hire follows the same five steps as Emma’s, plus a sponsorship layer. Teamed cannot sponsor a Skilled Worker visa from the EOR entity. UK Visas and Immigration require the sponsoring employer to be the same legal entity that signs the employment contract.

That means a Skilled Worker hire needs your own UK sponsor licence. Application takes 8 weeks for a standard service, a few days for priority service at extra fee. With the licence in hand, the timeline runs: Certificate of Sponsorship issued, visa application submitted, biometrics booked, Home Office decision (3 weeks standard, 5 working days priority), then travel and start. Plan 8 to 10 weeks from offer accept to start date. Standard salary thresholds, the immigration skills charge, the immigration health surcharge, and visa fees stack up to several thousand pounds per hire before the new joiner walks in.

For sponsored hires the Teamed UK specialist will brief you on the licence application and the salary threshold. For day-to-day employment of the visa-holder once the visa is granted, the EOR model works as normal.

When EOR stops being the right answer, UK crossover

Most companies graduate from EOR to their own UK entity at 15 to 20 UK employees. The UK is a lower-threshold crossover market than France or Germany. Companies House filing is cheap, PAYE is well-documented, and there is no works-council, AÜG-style licensing, or notarial-deed friction to slow the build. The graduation maths is straightforward: a fixed EOR fee per head versus your own UK payroll, HR, and statutory compliance overhead at scale.
