The day-one rights landed on 6 April 2026, and most US-built handbooks haven't caught up.

Paternity leave, unpaid parental leave, and Statutory Sick Pay are now day-one rights in the UK. No qualifying service required.

Statutory Sick Pay rose to £123.25 per week. The 3 waiting days are gone. The lower earnings limit is gone.

Aisha runs people ops at a 35-person Bristol startup. Every new hire from 6 April onwards is owed the new rights from their first shift. A handbook drafted in 2025 will quietly under-deliver until somebody complains.

The April 2026 day-one rights wave, in force now

  • Paternity leave · day-one right (no qualifying service)
  • Unpaid parental leave · day-one right
  • Statutory Sick Pay · day-one, 3 waiting days abolished, lower earnings limit removed
  • SSP rate · £123.25 per week (up from £118.75)
  • SMP / SPP / SAP / ShPP / PBP · £194.32 per week (or 90% of earnings, whichever is lower)
  • Collective redundancy protective award · doubled to 180 days' full pay per affected employee
  • Coming January 2027 · unfair-dismissal qualifying period drops from 2 years to 6 months (not day-one, despite reports)

Working hours and overtime

The statutory maximum is 48 hours per week averaged over a 17-week reference. Employees can opt out in writing, and that opt-out is included in Teamed's standard UK contract.

Standard rest entitlements: a 20-minute break on any shift over 6 hours, 11 hours between workdays, and 24 hours' weekly rest (or 48 hours per fortnight). There is no statutory overtime premium. Premium pay for nights, weekends, or extra hours is contractual, not legal. Source rules in the Working Time Regulations.

Employment contracts

Written terms must be issued on or before day one. Fixed-term contracts run alongside permanent and zero-hour models, with one constraint: an employee on continuous fixed-term contracts for 4 years or more automatically becomes permanent unless there is an objective business reason otherwise.

Teamed's standard UK contract supports all three engagement types and signs on behalf of Teamed Ltd as the legal employer. You receive a fresh written statement during onboarding, audited against the latest statutory requirements.

Probation period

There is no statutory maximum probation period in the UK as of May 2026. Most run 3 to 6 months, defined in the contract. The government is consulting on a 9-month cap, with implementation proposed for autumn 2026.

Even during probation, terminations should follow a fair procedure. Hold a probation review meeting before serving notice. Teamed's UK ops handles this end to end, with written documentation of performance discussions, agreed objectives, and the basis for any extension or dismissal.

Intellectual property

IP created by an employee in the course of employment is automatically owned by the employer in the UK. No special clause is needed for the default to bite.

Teamed's UK contract includes explicit assignment language anyway, to cover edge cases: work created on personal time using company resources, third-party deliverables, and similar. Consultants and contractors are different. They require explicit written assignment; the employee default does not extend to non-employees. Moral rights are waivable in writing, and the UK contract waives them by default for commissioned work.

Teamed Legal Operations
The decision to push so much of UK employment law through a single statute pulls the UK closer to continental Europe. It leaves less room for judicial discretion.

UK employment outcomes now turn on the wording of the statute, not the back-and-forth of precedent. For US-headquartered buyers expanding here, plan for compliance documentation, not case-by-case interpretation.

What benefits must you provide UK employees in 2026?

Statutory minimums: 5.6 weeks annual leave including bank holidays, day-one Statutory Sick Pay at £123.25 per week, day-one paternity leave, and 3% pension auto-enrolment.

Daniel is a developer at a Leeds fintech. His 28 days of statutory leave include the bank holidays. Offer him "25 days plus bank holidays" and he gets more than the floor. Offer him "20 days plus bank holidays" and you are short.

Annual leave

UK statutory annual leave is 5.6 weeks per year, which works out to 28 days for a standard 5-day worker. Unique to the UK among Teamed countries: annual leave and bank holidays are bundled into the same 28-day count. Employers can fold the bank holidays into the statutory minimum or grant them on top.

Carry-over of the EU-origin 4 weeks is permitted only where sickness or statutory leave prevented use, with 18 months to take. The remaining 1.6 weeks of UK-origin leave cannot be carried over without written agreement.

Sick leave

Statutory Sick Pay rose to £123.25 per week from 6 April 2026. The 3 waiting days are abolished. The lower earnings limit is gone. SSP now pays from day one and is available to every employee, regardless of earnings level.

Duration is up to 28 weeks per period of incapacity. Employer enhanced sick pay above the statutory floor is contractual, not legal. Employees self-certify for the first 7 days; a doctor's fit note is required from day 8.

Parental leave

Paternity leave and unpaid parental leave became day-one rights on 6 April 2026. Statutory Maternity, Paternity, Adoption, Shared Parental, and Parental Bereavement Pay all rose to £194.32 per week (or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower).

Maternity leave is up to 52 weeks, 26 of those paid. New for 2026: up to 52 weeks of unpaid leave for a surviving partner where a mother or primary adopter dies. Mothers can take up to 10 Keeping In Touch days during maternity leave without ending the leave period.

Public holidays

Public holidays in 2026: 8 in England and Wales, 9 in Scotland, 10 in Northern Ireland. No legal requirement for enhanced bank-holiday pay. Time-and-a-half or double time on a bank holiday is contractual.

Most UK employers count bank holidays toward the 28-day statutory leave entitlement, and Teamed's standard UK contract follows that convention. When a bank holiday falls on a weekend, a substitute weekday holiday is granted in lieu, usually the following Monday.

What are payroll taxes in the United Kingdom in 2026?

UK employer National Insurance is 15% on earnings above £5,000 per year. Pension auto-enrolment is 3% employer plus 5% employee on qualifying earnings.

For Emma's £60,000 Manchester role, that is roughly £8,250 of Employer NIC and £1,300 of pension on top of salary. The Employment Allowance can offset up to £10,500 of the annual NIC bill if you qualify.

Employer tax

The headline rate is 15% on earnings above the £5,000 secondary threshold (frozen until 2030-31). Eligible employers can offset up to £10,500 of the annual bill via the Employment Allowance; the old £100,000 eligibility cap was removed. Employers with a pay bill above £3 million pay the Apprenticeship Levy at 0.5% above a £15,000 allowance. Benefits in kind and PAYE Settlement Agreements run at the same 15% rate. Source rates on gov.uk.

Pension auto-enrolment is mandatory: 3% employer minimum plus 5% employee minimum on qualifying earnings between £6,240 and £50,270. Every PAYE filing, every Real-Time Information submission, and every NIC reconciliation lands itemised on the invoice. Salary, NIC, pension, statutory recoveries, and the Teamed fee appear as separate lines.

Individual tax

UK income tax bands for 2025-26 (frozen): 0% to the £12,570 personal allowance, 20% basic rate to £50,270, 40% higher rate to £125,140, 45% additional rate above that. The personal allowance tapers by £1 for every £2 earned over £100,000, vanishing at £125,140.

Employees pay 8% National Insurance between £12,570 and £50,270, and 2% above. Student loan and graduate-tax deductions apply where the employee has registered loans. Teamed PAYE handles every employee deduction, processes P45 documents on starters and leavers, and issues annual P60s. Cross-border employees with UK shadow payroll obligations are handled case by case (the usual trigger is 183 days of physical presence under double-tax treaty rules, plus an economic-employer analysis).

The fair-procedure rule catches every US employer in the UK.

UK termination is not at-will. Every dismissal needs a fair reason and a fair procedure on top of statutory notice.

Notice scales with service: 1 week from 1 month to 2 years, then 1 week per year, capped at 12 weeks after 12 years.

James in Glasgow lets a 4-year retail manager go without a documented performance process. The notice is right, the reason is real, and he still loses an unfair-dismissal claim because the procedure was missing.

The same fair-procedure rule sits over redundancy, capability, conduct, and "some other substantial reason" dismissals. Skip the steps and the dismissal is unfair even if the decision was correct.

Two UK quirks catch every US buyer.

First: annual leave bundles bank holidays into one 28-day count. There is no "PTO plus holidays" in UK statute.

Second: IR35 puts the tax-classification risk on you, the engager, not on the contractor.

A US founder hiring a London designer through her own limited company is the one HMRC chases for back-tax if the engagement looks employed in substance.

Big Ben

Bank holidays sit inside the 28-day statutory minimum, not on top of it. The UK is the only Teamed country where these aren't separate counts. Brief US buyers up front; most expect "PTO plus holidays" and end up reading the contract twice.

IR35 makes the engager liable for getting tax classification right on UK contractors engaged through their own limited companies. Getting it wrong costs back-tax, employer NIC, and penalties for up to 6 tax years. Teamed Guard and Teamed Protect are purpose-built for this. Guard layers misclassification cover on top of contractors you engage directly; Protect transfers the liability to Teamed by routing the engagement through us.

What are the UK statutory notice periods?
