What does a £60k UK hire actually cost?

Pay £60,000, spend roughly £75,500.

The loading is 25 percent. The two biggest lines are employer National Insurance at 15 percent and pension auto-enrolment at 3 percent. Statutory holiday is already in the gross.

Employer NIC: the 15% you don't see on the offer letter

You pay 15 percent on every pound of an employee's salary above £5,000 a year. No upper limit.

On a £60k salary that is £8,250. On £100k it is £14,250. On £150k it is £21,750. The rate is frozen until April 2030.

James runs a Glasgow retail floor with eight full-time staff on £35,000. His employer NIC bill is £36,000 a year before he has paid anyone a pension. He budgeted base salaries only.

Class 1 employer NIC is the cost nobody can engineer around. Every employer pays it on every pound of earnings above the £5,000 secondary threshold per employee per year. High earners cost proportionally more than their salary suggests because there is no upper cap.

The Employment Allowance offset

Eligible employers can offset up to £10,500 of their annual Class 1 NIC bill through the Employment Allowance. The £100,000 cap on eligibility was removed for 2025 to 2026, which broadened access. Teamed Ltd applies the Employment Allowance at the entity level. For clients hiring through Teamed, the saving shows up as line-item transparency on every invoice, not as a discretionary discount.

Apprenticeship Levy

Employers with an annual pay bill above £3 million pay the Apprenticeship Levy at 0.5 percent above a £15,000 allowance. For mid-market clients building UK teams through Teamed, this kicks in at around 40 to 50 UK employees on average tech salaries. Levy funds can be drawn down for apprenticeship training within 24 months.

Class 1A and Class 1B NIC on benefits

If you provide benefits in kind (private medical, company cars, gym memberships, even some employer-paid insurance), Class 1A NIC at 15 percent applies on the cash-equivalent value reported through the P11D return. Class 1B NIC applies to PAYE Settlement Agreements at the same rate.

Your in-house payroll specialist (a country specialist who sits inside Teamed Ltd, not a ticket queue) handles the quarter-end PAYE reconciliation, the P11D filings, and the Apprenticeship Levy threshold check. When HMRC writes, a named contact picks up the phone the same day.

The pension auto-enrolment maths

You contribute 3 percent minimum as the employer. Your employee contributes 5 percent.

Both apply only to "qualifying earnings" between £6,240 and £50,270. On a £60k salary your minimum cost is £1,321 a year.

Aisha is a 26-year-old engineer at a Bristol startup, your first UK hire. From day one of eligibility you enrol her in a qualifying scheme. Skip it and the Pensions Regulator levies fines that start at £400 and escalate per day.

Auto-enrolment is not optional. Every UK employer with at least one eligible jobholder must enrol them into a qualifying scheme on day one of eligibility and contribute at least the statutory minimum.

Qualifying earnings, the calculation band

The 3 percent / 5 percent minimum applies to qualifying earnings: gross pay between £6,240 (lower threshold) and £50,270 (upper threshold) for the 2025 to 2026 tax year. Salary above £50,270 sits outside the qualifying-earnings band. That is why a £60k hire and a £100k hire show similar pension lines.

What competitive UK packages actually pay

Statutory minimum (3 percent) is the floor. The mid-market average for tech and professional services is 5 to 8 percent employer match. Senior roles often see 10 percent and above as a retention lever. Statutory leave: the cost most US buyers miss

UK statutory leave totals 5.6 weeks of paid holiday plus statutory sick, parental, bereavement, and (from 6 April 2026) day-one paternity leave.

Holiday is already built into the gross salary you offer. Sick and parental pay are statutory minimums you underwrite as needed.

When Emma takes her statutory three weeks of summer leave, your monthly payroll line for her does not change. The £60,000 you signed already pays for it.

Holiday is the headline. Every UK worker is entitled to 5.6 weeks of paid annual leave (28 days for a five-day worker). The UK is unique among Teamed countries in that bank holidays are bundled into the 28-day count. There is no "PTO plus holidays" structure; the entitlement is one combined pot.

Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)

From 6 April 2026, SSP rose to £123.25 per week and became a day-one right. The three waiting days are gone and the lower earnings limit is removed. Most absences are short; the cost lands at a few hundred pounds per employee per year on average. Teamed runs SSP administration end to end.

Statutory Maternity, Paternity, Adoption, Shared Parental, and Parental Bereavement Pay

All five statutory family-pay rates moved to £194.32 per week from 6 April 2026 (or 90 percent of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower). The first six weeks of Statutory Maternity Pay are at 90 percent of earnings; the remainder is at the flat £194.32 rate for up to 33 weeks. Employers reclaim 92 percent from HMRC (103 percent for small employers under the Small Employers' Relief threshold).

Bereaved Partners Paternity Leave 2026

New for 2026: up to 52 weeks of unpaid leave for partners where a mother or primary adopter dies. Rare event; the cost when it triggers is one full year of cover.

Three costs nobody quotes you

Three lines sit outside the 120 to 125 percent loading and trip up first-time UK buyers.

IR35 contractor classification risk, statutory probation procedure costs, and Class 1A NIC on benefits that look "free" at quote stage.

IR35 contractor classification

Engaging UK contractors through their own limited companies puts the tax-status assessment on you, the engager, not the contractor, for medium and large clients. A misclassified contractor can mean back payroll tax, employer NIC, and HMRC penalties stretching across six tax years. The cost of getting it wrong is materially bigger than the cost of getting it right.

Take Aisha, the Bristol engineer above. Her former agency engaged her as a Ltd-co contractor for 14 months. When the audit hit, the agency owed £42,000 in back tax and NIC for that single engagement. Teamed Guard and Teamed Protect are purpose-built for this. Guard layers misclassification cover on top of direct contractor engagements. Protect transfers liability to Teamed by routing the engagement through us.

Probation procedure costs

UK probation is not a free pass to terminate. Even during probation, dismissals must follow fair procedure: written notice, opportunity to improve, documented review meeting. The cost is mostly time (manager hours, HR documentation), but the failure mode is real. A botched probation dismissal can become an unfair-dismissal claim post-April 2027, once the qualifying period drops to six months.

Class 1A NIC on benefits

Private medical, gym memberships, company cars, even employer-paid life cover above certain thresholds: these carry Class 1A NIC at 15 percent on the cash-equivalent value reported through P11D after the tax year. A £1,200-a-year private health policy adds £180 in Class 1A. Stacked across the team, this becomes material.
