## Information-gain statement

Kansas operates a dual overtime regime that most EOR providers and HR guides ignore: FLSA-covered employers must pay overtime after 40 hours, but employers NOT covered by the FLSA face the state threshold of **46 hours per week** under KSA 44-1204. This 6-hour gap is the operational trap. Neither Deel nor Rippling's state guides surface the statutory text explicitly. Kansas also has **no state meal or rest break law for adult employees** — the state minimum wage is $7.25 and has not moved above the federal floor. Final paychecks are due by the **next regular payday** (not "immediately" as buyers assume), with a 1%/day penalty after an 8-day grace period, capped at 100% of unpaid wages under KSA 44-315.
