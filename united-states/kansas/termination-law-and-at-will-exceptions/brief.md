## Answer Capsule (40–80 words)

Kansas is an at-will state — no notice period is legally required and no severance pay is mandated by statute. The Kansas Wage Payment Act (KSA 44-313 et seq.) sets the final paycheck deadline as the next regular payday for both fired and resigned employees, with a 1% daily wage penalty for wilful non-payment (up to 100% of unpaid wages). Three common-law and statutory exceptions limit the at-will rule: public policy (*Palmer v. Brown*, 1988), implied contract (*Morriss v. Coleman Co.*, 1987), and the Kansas Act Against Discrimination (KAAD, KSA 44-1001), which covers employers with just 4 employees — well below Title VII's 15-employee federal floor.
