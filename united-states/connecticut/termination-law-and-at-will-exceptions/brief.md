## Answer-first thesis
Connecticut is at-will but the doctrine layer is mid-to-broad: three judicial exceptions (public policy under Sheets, implied contract from handbook under Coelho/Torosyan, a partially recognised implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing limited to public-policy-style facts after Magnan v. Anaconda Industries). The cash mechanic is moderate-strict: Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-71c requires final pay by the next business day on involuntary discharge (not "immediately" like California or Colorado) and next regular payday for voluntary quits. Distinctive Connecticut layers: CFEPA (§ 46a-60, broader than Title VII, 3-employee threshold, 300-day CHRO filing window after PA 19-93), and § 31-51o — not a mass-layoff notice statute but an employer-paid 120-day healthcare continuation rule triggered by relocation/closing for 100+ employee establishments. Federal WARN applies on top.

## PROMPT FACT CORRECTIONS (verify-prompt-facts compliance)
The orchestrator prompt contained two factual errors. Both have been verified against primary sources and corrected in the draft. Flagged `factual_uncertainty:prompt_correction` in HIGH_CONCERN.md.

1. **Final paycheck deadline** — Prompt said "Conn Gen Stat § 31-71c — by next regular payday for both voluntary and involuntary. NOT immediate like CA/CO."
   - **Correction**: § 31-71c requires payment **by the next business day** for involuntary discharge (employer-initiated termination) and **by the next regular payday** for voluntary quit. Source: Connecticut Employment Law Blog (Jan 2026 article on timely final wage payments); LawServer text of § 31-71c; CT DOL Wage and Workplace Standards.
   - The prompt's "NOT immediate like CA/CO" framing is correct in spirit (Connecticut is not same-day) but the specific "next regular payday" rule applies only to voluntary quits and labor-dispute layoffs, not involuntary discharge.

2. **CT mini-WARN at § 31-51n** — Prompt said "WARN: federal floor + CT mini-WARN (Conn Gen Stat § 31-51n) for 100+ employee mass layoffs/relocations".
   - **Correction**: § 31-51n is the **Definitions** section of Chapter 557 Part II (covered establishment, employee, employer, relocation, closing). The substantive Connecticut state-law obligation is at **Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-51o**, which requires an employer of a covered establishment (100+ employees) that **relocates** or **closes** to **continue group health insurance coverage at the employer's expense for 120 days** or until the employee becomes eligible for other group coverage, whichever is sooner. This is **not** a federal-WARN-style 60-day advance-notice statute. Connecticut has no general state-level WARN advance-notice requirement; federal WARN is the only advance-notice statute that applies. Source: Justia 2020 and 2023 statutory text of § 31-51o; CT General Assembly Chapter 557 Part II.

These corrections were applied before drafting. The page treats § 31-51o accurately as a continuation-of-benefits statute rather than a notice statute.

## Key facts (verified, with sources)
- **At-will baseline** — judicial doctrine; *Sheets v. Teddy's Frosted Foods, Inc.*, 179 Conn. 471, 427 A.2d 385 (1980) recognised the public-policy exception. (Source: Justia, CT Supreme Court 1980)
- **Implied contract from handbook** — *Coelho v. Posi-Seal International, Inc.*, 208 Conn. 106, 544 A.2d 170 (1988); progressive-discipline or for-cause language in a handbook can become contractually binding; reaffirmed in *Torosyan v. Boehringer Ingelheim Pharmaceuticals* (1995). (Sources: Casemine, Lex Mundi, CGA OLR Report 95-R-1066)
- **Implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing** — *Magnan v. Anaconda Industries, Inc.*, 193 Conn. 558, 479 A.2d 781 (1984); the covenant exists in every Connecticut contract, but an at-will employee cannot maintain a stand-alone breach-of-covenant claim solely on the absence of just cause; the covenant attaches to discharge claims that would also support a public-policy *Sheets* claim. (Source: Wikipedia, vLex case law)
- **CFEPA** — Conn. Gen. Stat. § 46a-60 et seq.; broader than Title VII; covers **employers with 3+ employees** (vs Title VII's 15); CHRO is the enforcement agency; filing window is **300 days** following Public Act 19-93 (2019, effective 1 October 2019). (Sources: Legal Clarity; Connecticut Legal Services Authority; Remotelaws)
- **Final paycheck** — Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-71c. Involuntary: **next business day**. Voluntary: **next regular payday**. Labor-dispute layoffs: next regular payday. (Sources: CT DOL Form DOL-74; LawServer; Pullman & Comley; CBIA; FindLaw codified statute)
- **Wage-payment penalty** — Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-72; **double damages** plus costs and attorney's fees, unless the employer proves a genuine belief that the underpayment was lawful (then single damages plus costs and fees). (Source: CT Employment Law Blog, January 2026)
- **CT § 31-51o healthcare continuation** — Employers of covered establishments (**100+ employees**) that relocate or close must continue group health insurance coverage **at the employer's expense for up to 120 days**, or until the employee becomes eligible for other group coverage, whichever is sooner. Applies to full-time employees and those expected to work 20+ hours/week for at least 26 weeks in a 12-month period. (Sources: Justia 2020 and 2023 statutory text)
- **Federal WARN** — applies in Connecticut for 100+ FT employer; 60-day advance notice; 50+ at single site for plant closing or 50-499/33% or 500+ mass layoff. CT DOL Rapid Response Unit receives the notice (860-263-6580; dol.rapidresponse@ct.gov). (Sources: CT DOL Rapid Response page; CGA OLR Report 2019-R-0180)
- **Minimum wage 2026** — $16.94/hour effective 1 January 2026, indexed to Employment Cost Index. (Source: Shipman & Goodwin; cache)
- **Paid sick leave (HB 5005, effective 1 January 2024 expansion)** — Connecticut Paid Sick Leave Law expanded; 11+ employees as of 1 January 2026, all employers (1+) as of 1 January 2027. Accrual 1 hour per 30 worked, cap 40 hours/year, employee may use after 120 calendar days. (Source: Shipman & Goodwin; HR for Health)
- **CT PFML** — 0.5% employee-only contribution (100% employee-funded — unique in US); 2026 wage base $184,500 (or $176,100 if SSA finalises lower — see cache caveat); max annual $922.50; 12 weeks leave + 2 weeks pregnancy incapacity. (Source: ctpaidleave.org; CT 2026 compliance cache)
- **No statutory damage caps under CFEPA** — unlike Title VII, CFEPA does not impose statutory caps on compensatory and punitive damages tied to employer size. Back pay, front pay, emotional distress, punitive damages, attorney's fees all available. (Source: Connecticut Legal Services Authority guide)
