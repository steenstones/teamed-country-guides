## The Connecticut angle

Connecticut runs two state leave statutes that compound on top of the federal FMLA floor, and the entire stack is moving fast. **Connecticut Paid Leave (CT PFML / PFMLA)** under Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-49g et seq. is the only US paid-family-and-medical-leave programme funded **100% by employee contributions** (no employer match), which makes it cosmetically cheap on the employer P&L but operationally complex because the employer is the withholding agent. **Connecticut Paid Sick Leave** under Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-57r et seq. just went through the most significant expansion since the original 2011 statute: Public Act 24-8 phases coverage from 25-plus employees on 1 January 2025, to 11-plus on 1 January 2026, to all employers on 1 January 2027, and replaced the old "service worker" carve-out with near-universal coverage of all occupations except seasonal workers under 120 days. Most competitor content still cites the pre-2025 50-plus service-worker test and is materially stale.

The compliance question is not which programme to ship; it is how to administer two banks (sick + PFMLA), two portals (CT DOL ReEmployCT for sick-leave audits + CT Paid Leave Authority for PFMLA contributions), and one tenure-and-headcount matrix that changes again on 1 January 2027.

## Layout sections (Q-heading → answer-first)

1. **Which paid-leave laws apply** — "Which paid-leave laws apply to Connecticut employees in 2026?" → Three: CT Paid Leave (PFMLA), CT Paid Sick Leave, federal FMLA at 50-plus employees. Public Act 24-8 expanded the sick-leave coverage from 50-plus service workers to 11-plus all-occupation employers on 1 January 2026.
2. **CT Paid Sick Leave** — "How much paid sick leave does Connecticut require in 2026?" → 1 hour per 30 hours worked, capped at 40 hours per year, mandatory for every employer with 11 or more employees on 1 January 2026 (was 25-plus on 1 January 2025; will be every employer on 1 January 2027). Front-loading allowed in lieu of accrual. Carryover up to 40 hours.
3. **CT PFML (Paid Family and Medical Leave)** — "What is Connecticut Paid Family and Medical Leave and who pays it?" → 100% employee-paid 0.5% contribution on wages up to the Social Security wage base ($184,500 in 2026, max $922.50/year). 12 weeks of paid leave (14 if pregnancy incapacitation). Up to $1,016.40 weekly benefit (60× state minimum wage $16.94). Unique in the US — no employer match.
4. **Public Act 24-8 phase-in** — "How did Public Act 24-8 change Connecticut paid sick leave?" → Three-phase coverage expansion (25+ → 11+ → all employers); written-notice requirement at hire or 1 January 2025; three-year record-retention rule; usable for the employee, family member, mental health day, bereavement, and domestic violence safe leave.
5. **Stacking** — "How do CT Paid Sick Leave, CT PFML, and FMLA stack for a single pregnancy?" → Sick covers short predictable absences; PFMLA covers the 12-week wage-replacement event; FMLA runs concurrently with PFMLA at 75-plus-employee employers within 75 miles. Stack can hit ~5 months protected for a complicated pregnancy.
6. **Private plan opt-out** — "Can a Connecticut employer offer a private PFMLA plan instead?" → Yes, by demonstrating parity to CT Paid Leave Authority on benefit duration, weekly cap, qualifying reasons, and job protection. Structurally identical to New York PFL private-plan model. Annual reapproval. State plan wins for most.
