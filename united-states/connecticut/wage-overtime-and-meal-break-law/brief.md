## Key statutes verified
- **Minimum wage:** $16.94/hr from 1 Jan 2026 (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-58; Public Act 19-4 ECI indexation). 3.6% rise from 2025's $16.35. Announced by Gov. Lamont September 2025.
- **Tipped wages:** Hotel/restaurant service $8.23/hr (tip credit $8.71); bartender $6.38/hr (tip credit $10.56). Weekly written tip-credit attestation required.
- **Overtime:** Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-76c — 1.5x over 40 hrs in workweek. No daily OT. Mirrors federal FLSA.
- **Meal break:** Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-51ii — 30 consecutive minutes for shifts ≥ 7.5 consecutive hours. Must be taken after first 2 hrs and before last 2 hrs. Exemptions if employer provides 30+ min total paid rest/meal time within the 7.5-hour period.
- **Pay frequency:** Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-71b — default weekly OR every 2 weeks. Less frequent (semi-monthly, monthly) requires CT DOL approval on application. End of pay period no more than 8 days before payday.
- **Final pay on discharge:** Next business day after discharge (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-71c).
- **Class D felony for wage-payment violations** if unpaid total > $2,000 (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 31-71g).

## 2026 layer — warehouse worker quota law (SB 298 / PA 25-XX)
- Signed by Gov. Lamont 3 March 2026; effective 1 July 2026.
- Quotas may not interfere with statutory meal periods or bathroom use.
- Applies to 250+ employees at single distribution centre, or 1,000+ across multiple CT centres.
- Written quota disclosure to all current employees by 1 August 2026.
- Civil penalties: $1,000 / $2,000 / $3,000 escalating.

## Distinctive Connecticut angles (per row guidance)
- **Weekly pay frequency** — Connecticut is one of only a handful of US states with a default weekly-or-bi-weekly rule. Most states allow semi-monthly without state approval.
- **7.5-hour meal break trigger** — stricter than the federal default (no FLSA meal-break rule) and only ~7 states mandate adult meal breaks at all (CA, CO, KY, ME, MA, NV, OR, WA, plus CT).
- **No daily overtime** — contrast with neighbouring tier-1 states (California daily-8, Colorado daily-12).
