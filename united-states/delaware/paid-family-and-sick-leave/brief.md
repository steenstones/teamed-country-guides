## Statutory tiering — the headline nuance
Delaware is the first US state to apply a **graduated employer-size carve-out** to a state paid family-and-medical-leave programme. Most peer states (CA, CT, MA, NJ, NY, OR, RI, WA, DC) use universal employer coverage with no headcount carve-out for the contribution side. Delaware mirrors the federal FMLA pattern more closely:

| Employer headcount in Delaware | Contribution required | Benefits available to employees |
|---|---|---|
| <10 employees | None | None under HDFA (federal FMLA may still apply at 50+) |
| 10-24 employees | 0.32% (parental only) | Parental leave only (up to 12 weeks) |
| 25+ employees | 0.8% (full programme) | Parental + medical + family + military (12 weeks combined) |
