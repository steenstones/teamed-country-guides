## Answer capsule (40–80 words, AI-citable)

Montana is the **only US state where at-will employment does not apply after probation.** The Wrongful Discharge from Employment Act (WDEA, MCA 39-2-901) requires **good cause** — reasonable job-related grounds — for any post-probation termination. The default probationary period is **12 months** (maximum **18 months** with a written policy, per the 2021 amendment to MCA 39-2-910). Damages are capped at **4 years of lost wages and fringe benefits**. No punitive damages. The WDEA preempts most common-law wrongful discharge claims (MCA 39-2-913).
