## Answer Capsule (40-80 words)

Michigan classifies workers under three separate frameworks: the IRS 20-factor common-law control test (MCL 421.42, effective January 2013) for Unemployment Insurance purposes, the IRS three-category control test for income-tax withholding, and the Michigan Construction Industry Worker Classification Act (2017) for the construction sector. No ABC test exists in Michigan. Intentional misclassification under pending Senate Bills 6 and 7 carries up to a $10,000 fine and a two-year felony for repeat offenders.

## Counter-narrative (sourced angle)

Michigan is the state where employers discover the hard way that "no ABC test" doesn't mean "no test." Three frameworks run simultaneously: if you get the UI test right and the withholding test wrong, you owe back taxes. If you get both right but your worker is in construction, a third statute kicks in. The only way to eliminate the classification decision entirely is to put the worker on payroll via an EOR — then the IRS common-law test answers itself.
