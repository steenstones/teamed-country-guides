## Key editorial angle
Michigan is a "ESTA-only" state. No PFML. The ESTA is notable because it launched twice (2018 initiative → lame-duck amendment → Supreme Court reinstatement → HB 4002 amendment on effective date). The version employers must comply with today is the HB 4002 version: 72 hours for 11+ employees, 40 hours for 10 or fewer. The absence of PFML is a structural feature, not a gap about to close, and is a meaningful talent-strategy consideration.

## Compliance facts (from michigan.json cache)

### ESTA
- Statute: Michigan Earned Sick Time Act, Public Act 2 of 2025
- Effective: 21 February 2025
- Replaces: Paid Medical Leave Act (PMLA)
- Accrual: 1 hour per 30 hours worked
- Large employer (11+ employees): up to 72 hours paid per year, 72-hour carryover cap
- Small employer (10 or fewer employees): up to 40 hours paid per year, 40-hour carryover cap
- Waiting period: up to 120 calendar days before use (accrual starts day 1)
- Frontloading: permitted
- Small employer extended deadline: 1 October 2025
- New business transition: 3 years from first hire, or until 11+ employees reached (whichever comes first)

### HB 4002
- Signed: 21 February 2025 (same day as ESTA effective date)
- Key changes: two-tier cap (72/40), extended small-employer deadline, new business transition
- Did NOT change: universal coverage, 1:30 accrual, 120-day waiting period option, frontloading option, qualifying reasons, anti-retaliation, notice/documentation rules
- 90-hour cap: never enacted — was original initiative text, not HB 4002 enacted version

### State PFML
- Does not exist in Michigan
- No employer payroll contribution
- No state wage-replacement benefit for parental/family-care leave
- Federal FMLA (unpaid, 12 weeks) at 50+ employees is the only statutory protection for longer leave events

### Federal FMLA
- 50+ employees within 75-mile radius
- 12-month service + 1,250 hours eligibility
- 12 weeks unpaid job-protected per 12-month period (26 for military caregiver)
- Health coverage continues during leave

### Pregnancy / parental accommodation
- ELCRA (Michigan Elliott-Larsen Civil Rights Act): all employers 1+, no discrimination, reasonable accommodation
- Federal PWFA: 15+ employers, explicit reasonable-accommodation obligation for pregnancy-related limitations
- Federal PUMP Act: nursing accommodation for 1 year after birth
- No standalone Michigan paid parental leave statute
