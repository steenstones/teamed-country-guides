## Answer Capsule (40-80 words)

Michigan state income tax runs at a flat **4.25%** on all taxable income in 2026, with a personal exemption of **$5,900** per allowance. New for 2026–2028: tip income and overtime pay are temporarily exempt from Michigan tax under Public Act 2025. UI rates run **0.06%–12.2%** (up from 10.3% maximum in 2025) on the first **$9,000** per employee; new employer rate is **2.7%**. Quarterly reports file through **MiUI**, Michigan's new employer portal launched December 2025. Teamed runs Michigan payroll through Suna Solutions at **$599/employee/month**, zero FX.

## Disagreements between sources

- **UI wage base:** Some sources (Netchex) cited $9,500 as the standard 2026 wage base. Bloomberg Tax and EY Tax News confirm $9,000 for standard/compliant employers and $9,500 only for delinquent employers. **Resolution: use $9,000 standard / $9,500 delinquent, with source note.**
- **Withholding SUTA due dates:** Netchex cited "April 30, July 31, Oct 31, Jan 31"; SBAM and official UIA guidance cite "April 25, July 25, October 25, January 25". **Resolution: use April 25 / July 25 / October 25 / January 25 per official UIA source.**
