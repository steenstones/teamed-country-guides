> Most "Kentucky paid leave" summaries stop at "no state PFL, federal FMLA only." This page surfaces KRS 337.015 — the Kentucky statute that treats **accrued but unused vacation pay as earned wages** on termination, meaning an employer who writes a "use it or lose it" vacation policy without an explicit lump-sum-on-termination exclusion may owe the full accrued balance as wages. That is the statutory quirk that makes the Kentucky leave picture unique among sibling no-mandate states. No competitor page connects this to the leave-design conversation explicitly.

### Kentucky-specific angles (differentiation)

1. **KRS 337.015 vacation-as-wages**: Kentucky specifically classifies accrued vacation as earned wages under KRS 337.015. An employer with a "use it or lose it" policy who fails to explicitly exclude lump-sum payout on termination can be required to pay out accrued vacation at separation. This is the lever that makes leave policy design in Kentucky consequential in a way it isn't in true at-will states without this statutory hook.

2. **No local PSL ordinances**: Unlike some no-mandate states that still have city-level PSL rules (e.g. Pittsburgh, PA), Kentucky has no city-level paid sick leave ordinances. Louisville, Lexington, and other Kentucky cities have no municipal paid sick leave rules.

3. **Kentucky Civil Rights Act (KCRA)**: State-level discrimination protection for pregnancy/disability separate from (and sometimes broader in employment reach than) federal law.
