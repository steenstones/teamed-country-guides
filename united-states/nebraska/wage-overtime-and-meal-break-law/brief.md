## Page purpose

Answers the buyer query "Nebraska wage and overtime law 2026" for HR/finance leads at US, UK, and European companies hiring remote employees in Nebraska. The differentiating fact: Nebraska is not a federal-floor state. It doubled the federal minimum wage through two ballot initiatives (2022 + 2024) and now sits at $15.00/hr — one of the 15 highest state floors in the country. Most out-of-state employers haven't updated their pay bands.

## Key statutory facts (all provisional — verify against NE DOL before publish)

| Fact | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum wage (2026) | $15.00/hr from 1 Jan 2026 | Initiative 433 (Raise the Wage Act, Nov 2022 ballot) |
| Tipped cash wage | $7.50/hr (tip credit up to $7.50) | Initiative 433; Neb. Rev. Stat. § 48-1203.01 |
| Overtime trigger | 40 hrs/week (FLSA only; no daily OT, no double-time) | Federal FLSA; no separate NE statute |
| Adult meal/rest break mandate | None | No Nebraska statute |
| Minor breaks | 30-min break for shifts over 8 hrs on school days | NE child labour statutes + FLSA |
| Pay frequency | At least semi-monthly or bi-weekly | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 48-1212 |
| Final pay deadline | Next regular payday or within 2 weeks, whichever sooner | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 48-1230 |
| Paid sick leave | 1 hr/30 hrs worked; 56-hr cap (20+ employees); 40-hr cap (11–19) | Initiative 436 / Nebraska Healthy Families and Workplaces Act, eff. 1 Oct 2025; amended LB 415 |
| 2027 rate | $15.00 + CPI adjustment (to be announced NE DOL autumn 2026) | Initiative 433 CPI escalator |
| Exempt salary floor | $684/week federal (proposed $1,128 vacated Nov 2024) | 29 CFR Part 541 |

## Narrative angle

Nebraska's wage story is the ballot box outrunning the employer. Voters set a four-year glide path and most employers tracked it. But every phase step (2023, 2024, 2025, 2026) is a separate back-wage exposure date for anyone who missed an update. The 2024 sick-leave initiative means the trajectory is still moving. The page is framed around this "Nebraska is not a federal-floor state" surprise, using a named scenario (Jaylen, warehouse associate in Omaha) to make the exposure concrete.
