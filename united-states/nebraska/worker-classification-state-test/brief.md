## Key statutory facts (verified from cache + spec)

### Classification framework

| Test | Purpose | Statute |
|---|---|---|
| IRS 20-factor common-law | State income tax withholding + workers' comp | IRS Rev. Rul. 87-41; no separate NE test |
| NDOL 10-factor right-to-control | Unemployment insurance | Neb. Rev. Stat. § 48-604(5) |
| Employee Classification Act | Construction + delivery services | Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 48-2701 to 48-2716 |
| No ABC test | General classification (unlike CA, MA, NJ) | N/A |

### Employee Classification Act

- Sectors: construction services and delivery services
- Presumption: employee status is presumed in covered sectors (§ 48-2705)
- Independent contractor criteria: free from direction in fact; bona fide business with insurance + other clients; work outside hirer's core business (§ 48-2703)
- Civil violation: Class III violation (§ 48-2710)
- Criminal exposure: wilful misclassification is a criminal misdemeanour (§ 48-2712)
- Interagency coordination: NDOL shares findings with DOR and Workers' Comp Court (§ 48-2713)

### UI cost exposure on misclassification

- UI wage base: $9,000 (standard, 2026)
- New employer rate: 1.25% (non-construction)
- State income tax top rate: 4.55% (2026)
- Workers' comp: physical-labour rate codes materially higher than knowledge-work codes
