## Page thesis

Alaska is one of only two US states (with California) where daily overtime is mandatory. AS 23.10.060 requires 1.5× the regular rate for hours over 8 in a day OR over 40 in a week, whichever produces the higher dollar number for the employee, with an anti-pyramiding rule so no hour is counted twice. Layered on top: a $13.00/hr minimum wage from 1 January 2026 (rising to $14 on 1 July and $15 in July 2027 per Ballot Measure 1), a complete prohibition on the federal tip credit (Alaska is one of seven states), and an AS 23.10.350 break entitlement for any worker under 18 who hits 5+ consecutive hours. The page leads with the multi-pronged employer surprises (daily OT + no tip credit) and treats the 4-employee small-business exemption and the voluntary flex-plan as practical ways to manage the daily-overtime exposure.
