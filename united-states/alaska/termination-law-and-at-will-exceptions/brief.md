## Search intent + page job
Buyer is an HR / People Ops / founder hiring into Alaska who has heard "Alaska is at-will" and assumes the same loose at-will rules they get in Alabama or Texas. The real answer: Alaska is technically at-will, but it is one of the **broadest exception regimes in the US** — it recognises all three judicial exceptions (public policy, implied contract, AND implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing). On top of that, Alaska's **final paycheck rule is among the strictest** in the country: 3 working days when the employer terminates. The page job is to reframe "Alaska is at-will" as "Alaska is at-will-in-name-only — terminate carefully or the implied covenant of good faith claim is sitting on your desk in 30 days."

## Answer-First headline data points (use throughout)

### The three exceptions (the citation gold)
- **Public-policy exception**: *Kinzel v. Discovery Drilling, Inc.*, 93 P.3d 427 (Alaska 2004) — termination for exercising legal rights, refusing illegal acts, or whistleblowing is actionable in tort.
- **Implied-contract exception**: *Eales v. Tanana Valley Medical-Surgical Group, Inc.*, 663 P.2d 958 (Alaska 1983) — oral or written promises (offer letter, handbook) can convert at-will into for-cause employment for a definite term. Reinforced by *Jones v. Central Peninsula General Hospital*, 779 P.2d 783 (Alaska 1989) — handbooks can modify at-will.
- **Implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing**: *Mitford v. de Lasala*, 666 P.2d 1000 (Alaska 1983) and *Miller v. Safeway, Inc.*, 170 P.3d 655, 658-59 (Alaska 2007) — every Alaska at-will contract carries an implied covenant; breach by disparate treatment or termination violating public policy is actionable.

### Statutes
- **Final paycheck — employer-terminated**: AS 23.05.140(b) — within **3 working days** after termination (excluding the day of termination, weekends, and state/bank holidays). Penalty: regular wage for each day from demand until paid, capped at 90 working days.
- **Final paycheck — employee resignation**: AS 23.05.140(b) — next regular payday that is at least 3 days after notice.
- **Personnel-file access**: AS 23.10.430 — employee can inspect and copy at reasonable cost.
- **Workers' comp retaliation**: AS 23.30.247 — discharge in retaliation for filing a workers' comp claim is prohibited.
- **AKOSH whistleblower**: AS 18.60.089 — retaliation for safety reports prohibited (administered by Alaska Occupational Safety and Health).
- **Public-employee whistleblower**: AS 39.90.100 et seq. — protects state and political-subdivision employees reporting matters of public concern.
- **Jury duty**: AS 09.20.037 — discharge for jury service prohibited.

### Discrimination filing window
- Alaska State Commission for Human Rights (ASCHR) under **AS 18.80** — file within **300 days** of the alleged act. Covers employers with 1+ employees (state law) vs federal Title VII at 15+.
- ASCHR has a work-sharing agreement with the EEOC: the federal 180-day window is extended to **300 days** because Alaska is a deferral state. Cross-filing happens automatically; file once with either agency.

### Federal floor (always applies)
- Title VII (15+), ADA (15+), ADEA (20+), USERRA (1+), FMLA (50+ within 75 miles), FLSA retaliation.

### Mass layoff
- **Federal WARN Act only** — 29 U.S.C. § 2102, 100+ FT employees, 60 days advance notice. Alaska has **no state mini-WARN Act**. State Rapid Response coordination via Alaska Department of Labor and Workforce Development is encouraged, not required.

## Conflicting / uncertain data
None material. AS 23.05.140 is unambiguous on the 3-working-day rule (employer termination) and next-payday rule (employee quit). Case-law citations cross-checked against Justia and ALFA International's Labor & Employment Compendium.
