## Page summary

Minnesota runs two separate statutory classification tests. For non-construction workers, Minn. Stat. § 181.722, subd. 3 applies a 5-factor test (all five must be met). For construction workers, § 181.723, subd. 4 applies a 14-factor test expanded effective 1 March 2025, with a rebuttable presumption of employee status. Both tests are enforced by the Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry (DLI) under the Minnesota Misclassification Act, independently of any federal IRS common-law analysis. Civil penalties reach up to $10,000 per misclassified worker (increased effective 1 March 2025), plus back wages, back SUI premiums (DEED), back ESST accrual, and back PFML contributions.
