What is Minnesota Paid Family and Medical Leave?

One platform handles the entire stack: payroll, contracts, taxes, and compliance, no third-party tools to stitch together.Minnesota PFML is a state-run insurance programme that pays employees a portion of their wages when they take qualifying family or medical leave. Employers and employees both contribute a premium starting 1 January 2026.

Benefits and premium collection both launched on the same date: 1 January 2026. This is notable because most US state PFML programmes have a gap year between premium collection and benefit payment. In Minnesota, you owed the premium and employees could claim benefits from day one of the new year.

The programme is administered by the Minnesota Department of Employment and Economic Development (DEED). Employees apply directly through the state system; you do not manage claims in-house. Your obligations are to collect and remit the premium each quarter and to provide job protection during leave.

What reasons qualify for Minnesota PFML?

Minnesota PFML covers two leave categories: family leave (bonding, caregiving, military exigency, safety) and medical leave (employee's own serious health condition). The same 20-week annual cap applies across both.

Family leave qualifying reasons include: bonding with a new child (birth, adoption, or placement placement); caring for a family member with a serious health condition; qualifying exigency arising from a family member's military deployment; and safety leave for situations involving domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking.

Medical leave qualifying reasons include: the employee's own serious health condition or injury, including conditions arising from pregnancy.

The 20-week combined cap means an employee who takes 12 weeks for bonding after a birth can then take up to 8 further weeks of medical leave in the same benefit year, but not the full 12. Both the employer and employee contribute to the same fund regardless of which leave type is taken.

Minnesota PFML and federal FMLA cover many of the same qualifying events. Where they overlap, leave runs concurrently. FMLA provides 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year. MN PFML adds paid income replacement. For employees, the combination means income protection during what would otherwise be unpaid FMLA leave.

Does the small employer exemption affect the PFML premium?

Employers with 30 or fewer employees who also meet an average-wage condition pay a reduced premium of 75% of the standard rate. That brings the total from 0.88% down to approximately 0.66% of covered wages.

The wage condition: the employer's average employee wages must be at or below 150% of the Minnesota statewide average wage. Most small employers below the headcount threshold will meet this condition. Both criteria must be satisfied to access the reduced rate.

The small employer rate is not a full exemption. You still owe PFML contributions and must provide job protection during leave. The 75% rate reduces cost, it does not eliminate it.

Private plan option: You can substitute a private insurance plan for the state PFML programme. The private plan must meet or exceed state-plan benefits and receive DEED approval before the start of the plan year. If your existing disability or parental leave programme already covers equivalent benefits, getting it approved as a private plan lets you avoid contributing to the state fund. What sick leave must Minnesota employers provide?

Minnesota Earned Sick and Safe Time (ESST) requires all employers to provide paid sick leave at 1 hour for every 30 hours worked, up to 48 hours per year. It applies to every Minnesota employer, with no headcount threshold.

ESST has applied since 1 January 2024. The maximum annual accrual cap is 48 hours, but accrued hours carry over year-to-year until the employee's total balance reaches 80 hours. You do not have to pay out unused ESST on termination; the carry-over simply persists until the 80-hour ceiling.

From 1 January 2026, you may front-load ESST hours at the start of the year based on projected accrual, rather than tracking per-hour accrual throughout the year. This simplifies administration. If your advance falls short of the employee's actual accrual during the year, you must true up the balance before year end.

What ESST covers: the employee's own illness, injury, or preventive care; a family member's care needs or preventive appointments; school closures due to public health emergencies; and safety-related absences (domestic abuse, sexual assault, or stalking involving the employee or a family member).

Local ordinances: Minneapolis had its own sick time ordinance before ESST. ESST sets the state floor; Minneapolis's ordinance may be more generous in specific respects. Teamed tracks the applicable local floor for each employee's work location. Source: MN DLI, Earned Sick and Safe Time.

How do PFML and ESST interact?

PFML and ESST are separate programmes with separate accrual, separate qualifying reasons, and separate benefit structures. They can run concurrently in some circumstances but do not replace each other.

ESST covers short-term paid sick time funded entirely by the employer through payroll practice. PFML is an insurance programme where both employer and employee pay premiums, and the state pays benefits on a wage-replacement basis during qualifying leave.

An employee who takes PFML for their own medical condition may also use ESST days during a waiting period or for days not covered by PFML. You may require employees to use accrued ESST time during PFML leave, effectively topping up the PFML benefit to full pay, but you cannot require it beyond what your written leave policy states.

For tracking purposes: PFML is tracked by DEED (the state) and reported quarterly by employers. ESST is tracked by you (or your EOR) as part of the payroll record. Teamed's platform tracks both separately per employee and generates the correct payslip line items for each.

What notice and posting obligations apply to Minnesota PFML?

Minnesota employers must post the PFML notice poster, include PFML information in new-hire packets, and notify employees of their PFML rights when a qualifying event occurs. Job protection for the duration of approved leave is mandatory.

The Minnesota DEED issues a mandatory PFML employer poster that must be displayed in a place accessible to all employees. For fully remote teams, electronic posting satisfies the requirement. The poster is available at paidleave.mn.gov.

Job protection: employees returning from PFML-qualifying leave have the right to be restored to the same or an equivalent position, with the same pay, benefits, and terms and conditions of employment. Retaliation against an employee for taking PFML leave is prohibited.

Interaction with other MN leave laws: Minnesota has a separate pregnancy and parental leave law (Minn. Stat. § 181.941) providing up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave for birth, adoption, or placement placement. With PFML now providing paid leave for the same events, the unpaid law remains on the books but most employees will apply for PFML benefits rather than relying on the unpaid right alone.

What should you know before hiring in Minnesota in 2026?

Minnesota has four overlapping leave obligations in 2026: federal FMLA (unpaid), MN Pregnancy/Parental Leave (unpaid), MN PFML (new, paid), and ESST (sick time). Understanding how they stack matters for leave policies and payroll setup.

Three things that catch first-time Minnesota employers in 2026. First, PFML launched with benefits and premiums on the same date. If you hired a Minnesota employee after 1 January 2026 without PFML in your payroll setup, you owe back premiums for Q1 (due 30 April 2026) and Q2 (due 31 July 2026). That is not a forward-looking problem, it is a current one.

Second, the premium split is not legally required to be 50/50 beyond the minimum employer floor. You must contribute at least 0.44%, but you could choose to pay the full 0.88% as a benefit. Some employers absorb the employee share to simplify payslips for remote workers who may be unfamiliar with the new deduction. The decision belongs in your written leave policy, not just your payroll configuration.

Third, Minnesota has no state disability insurance (SDI) programme separate from PFML. Unlike California, New York, or New Jersey, you do not have a parallel SDI obligation to manage. Total short-term leave cost in Minnesota is more predictable as a result.

ESST has been in force since January 2024. If you have Minnesota employees hired before then who have never had ESST tracked, you may have an accrual liability. Source: MN DLI, ESST.

Frequently asked questions about Minnesota family and sick leave

When did Minnesota Paid Family and Medical Leave go live?

Minnesota PFML benefit payments and premium collection both started on 1 January 2026. The first quarterly premium payment (covering Q1 2026 wages) was due 30 April 2026. Source: paidleave.mn.gov.

What is the Minnesota PFML premium rate in 2026?

The total MN PFML premium is 0.88% of covered wages in 2026, applied against a $185,000 wage base. The default split is 50/50: employer pays at least 0.44% and may deduct up to 0.44% from employee pay. Employers may pay up to 100% of the premium voluntarily. Source: paidleave.mn.gov.

How many weeks of leave does Minnesota PFML provide?

Employees can take up to 12 weeks of family leave and up to 12 weeks of medical leave per benefit year, with a hard cap of 20 combined weeks. The maximum weekly benefit is $1,423 in 2026. Source: paidleave.mn.gov.

What is Minnesota Earned Sick and Safe Time (ESST)?

Minnesota ESST requires all employers to provide paid sick leave at 1 hour per 30 hours worked, up to 48 hours per year. Accrued hours carry over year-to-year until the balance reaches 80 hours. ESST applies to every Minnesota employer regardless of size, effective 1 January 2024. Statute: Minn. Stat. §§ 181.9445–.9448. Source: MN DLI, dli.mn.gov.

Can a small employer in Minnesota get a reduced PFML premium?

Yes. Employers with 30 or fewer employees whose average wages are at or below 150% of the statewide average wage qualify for a reduced premium equal to 75% of the standard rate, bringing the total to approximately 0.66%. Source: paidleave.mn.gov.

Does Minnesota PFML replace other leave laws like FMLA?

Minnesota PFML runs alongside federal FMLA, not in addition to it for the same qualifying event. Where FMLA and MN PFML cover the same leave, they run concurrently. FMLA provides unpaid job protection; MN PFML adds paid income replacement during that same period. Source: paidleave.mn.gov.
