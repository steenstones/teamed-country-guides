## Key compliance facts (from compliance cache)

- **At-will rule:** Missouri is at-will. Public-policy exception confirmed in *Fleshner v. Pepose Vision Institute*, 304 S.W.3d 81 (Mo. banc 2010). Four categories only: refusing illegal act, reporting violations, exercising statutory right, performing public duty.
- **Final pay — involuntary:** Due immediately at separation (RSMo § 290.110). Not next regular payday.
- **Final pay — voluntary resignation:** Due next regular payday (RSMo § 290.110).
- **Final pay penalty:** Written demand via certified mail → if unpaid 7 days → up to 60 days additional wages at same daily rate.
- **Service Letter Statute (RSMo § 290.140):** Request-driven. Employee with 90+ days of service, involuntarily separated, may request written letter. Must state: nature of work, duration, true reason for dismissal. False/misleading letter → actual and punitive damages.
- **MHRA threshold:** 6 employees (RSMo § 213 et seq.). Below Title VII (15), ADA (15), ADEA (20).
- **No statutory severance required.**
- **No state advance notice requirement** for at-will terminations.
- **WARN Act:** Federal only (no Missouri state equivalent). 60-day notice for 50+ layoffs at 100+ headcount employers.

---

## Differentiation angle

Missouri's Service Letter Statute (RSMo § 290.140) is the page's editorial hook. It is genuinely unusual — few other states have a comparable obligation, and it is consistently missed by out-of-state employers and national payroll providers. The "true reason for dismissal" element creates specific liability when documentation and verbal reasons diverge. This is the angle that earns citation and makes the page distinctive versus generic EOR competitors who summarise at-will and stop.
