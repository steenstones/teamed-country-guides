Does Missouri have a mandatory sick leave law in 2026?

Prop A was in force
1 May – 27 Aug 2025. Then HB 567 repealed it

Missouri voters passed Proposition A in November 2024. Prop A added a state earned sick time law to the Missouri Revised Statutes (RSMo 290.600) alongside the minimum wage increase. Under Prop A, employers were required to provide one hour of earned paid sick time for every 30 hours worked, beginning 1 May 2025. Two annual caps applied: 40 hours per year for small employers (fewer than 15 employees) and 56 hours per year for larger employers. Unused time carried over up to 80 hours.

The Missouri Legislature passed HB 567 in the 2025 session. Governor Mike Kehoe signed HB 567 on 10 July 2025. RSMo 290.600 (the earned sick time statute) was repealed effective 28 August 2025. The bill lacked an emergency clause, creating a 17-week window during which Prop A obligations were in force. HB 567 did not enact a replacement sick leave mandate. After 28 August 2025, Missouri employers are not required to provide any paid sick leave under state law.

The repeal does not affect any sick leave policies employers voluntarily offered before Prop A took effect, or any voluntary enhancements beyond Prop A that employers adopted. If your company policy provided paid sick leave before May 2025, that policy remains in place under its own terms; HB 567 simply removed the floor. Teamed audited every Missouri employee's leave setup at the August 2025 repeal and aligned employer documentation accordingly.

Two compliance points remain worth noting. First, for any employer who had Missouri employees between 1 May and 27 August 2025: you were legally required to provide Prop A sick leave during that window. If you did not, that is a historic exposure. Second, the Missouri DOL did not issue definitive guidance on whether unused Prop A accruals had to be paid out or honoured post-repeal; the Missouri Chamber of Commerce FAQ advised employers to review their own policies and any CBA terms. If you have ongoing Missouri hires who accrued Prop A leave in that window and your policy was silent on payout, legal review is warranted.

How does federal FMLA apply to Missouri employers?

Federal FMLA applies to Missouri employers with 50 or more employees within 75 miles of a worksite. Eligible employees get up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year. Missouri has no state supplement to expand FMLA coverage.

Because Missouri has no state family or medical leave law, FMLA is the entire statutory safety net for employees who need extended leave. For employers under the 50-employee threshold, there is no statutory leave entitlement beyond what they voluntarily provide. A 10-person Missouri remote team has no leave obligations beyond what their employment contract specifies.

FMLA qualifying events cover: birth, adoption, or placement of a child in kinship care; the serious health condition of the employee; the serious health condition of a spouse, child, or parent; and qualifying military exigency. All 12 weeks are unpaid unless the employer requires (or the employee elects) to run PTO concurrently.

No. As of this review date, neither Kansas City nor St. Louis has enacted a local mandatory paid sick or family leave ordinance that applies to private-sector employers.

Missouri's two largest cities do not have standalone paid sick leave laws for private employers. The St. Louis City government updated its own internal family and medical leave policy for city employees in 2022, but this is an internal HR policy for municipal workers, not an ordinance that binds private employers operating in St. Louis.

The absence of local leave ordinances in Kansas City and St. Louis is partly a function of Missouri's legislative history. With the state law now repealed, whether Kansas City or St. Louis will pursue local ordinances is an open question; neither city had active ordinance proceedings as of this review date.

What should out-of-state companies offer for sick and parental leave when hiring in Missouri?

The repeal of Prop A and the absence of state PFML give Missouri employers unusual flexibility, but that flexibility cuts both ways. A Missouri hire can compare your offer against California or New York employers where state law provides a benefit floor. Frequently asked questions about Missouri paid family and sick leave

Does Missouri have a paid family leave law in 2026?

No. Missouri has no state-funded paid family and medical leave programme and no state payroll tax to fund leave benefits. Employees rely on federal FMLA (unpaid, employers with 50+ employees) and any employer-provided voluntary benefits. There is no Missouri maternity pay or paternity pay entitlement under state law.

Does Missouri have a mandatory sick leave law in 2026?

No. Missouri voters passed Proposition A in November 2024 adding a state earned sick time law (RSMo 290.600, effective 1 May 2025). The Missouri Legislature passed HB 567 and Governor Kehoe signed it on 10 July 2025, repealing the statute effective 28 August 2025. There is no replacement sick leave mandate. Employers were required to provide paid sick leave only during the 17-week window from 1 May to 27 August 2025.

What was Missouri Proposition A and what happened to it?

Proposition A was a November 2024 ballot initiative that raised Missouri's minimum wage and added a state earned sick time law. The sick time provision required 1 hour of paid sick leave per 30 hours worked, with annual caps of 40 hours (employers with fewer than 15 employees) and 56 hours (employers with 15 or more). It took effect 1 May 2025 but was repealed by HB 567 effective 28 August 2025. The $15.00/hour minimum wage from 1 January 2026 remains in effect; Prop A's CPI indexing for 2027 and beyond was eliminated by HB 567.

How does FMLA apply to employers hiring in Missouri?

Federal FMLA applies to employers with 50 or more employees within a 75-mile radius of any worksite. Eligible employees (12 months tenure, 1,250 hours worked in the prior year) may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for birth or adoption of a child, the employee's serious health condition, or a family member's serious health condition. Missouri has no state supplement that expands FMLA coverage or makes any portion of it paid.

Do Kansas City or St. Louis have local paid sick leave laws?

No. As of this review date (30th May 2026), neither Kansas City nor St. Louis has enacted a local paid sick or family leave ordinance that applies to private-sector employers. The St. Louis city government updated its own internal employee leave policy in 2022, but that is not a private employer mandate. Both cities retain the authority to enact local ordinances; employers with workers in either city should monitor local legislative activity.
