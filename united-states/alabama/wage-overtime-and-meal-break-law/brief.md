## Page thesis

Alabama defers to federal law on nearly every wage-and-hour question. The state has no minimum wage, no state overtime, no mandated adult meal or rest breaks, and is preempted from local minimum-wage ordinances by Alabama Code § 25-7-41. The page leads with that practical employer point, then walks through every line a US payroll team has to apply: federal $7.25 floor, FLSA 40-hour overtime, the FLSA "engaged to wait" rule, and the one carve-out where Alabama law adds a positive requirement on top of federal — the documented 30-minute meal break for minors aged 14-15 who work more than 5 consecutive hours.

## Key data points (with source URLs)

| Fact | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Federal minimum wage | $7.25/hour | dol.gov — Fair Labor Standards Act |
| Alabama state minimum wage | None — defers to federal | Alabama Department of Labor; § 25-7-41 |
| Local minimum wage preemption | Statewide ban on city/county ordinances exceeding state/federal | Alabama Code § 25-7-41 (2016) |
| FLSA overtime threshold | 40 hours / workweek, 1.5x regular rate | dol.gov — Fair Labor Standards Act |
| Daily overtime | None in Alabama | (negative confirmation) |
| FLSA exempt salary basis | $684/week ($35,568/year) | dol.gov — 29 CFR 541 |
| Adult meal break required | None | Alabama Department of Labor |
| Adult rest break required | None | Alabama Department of Labor |
| Short breaks <20 min | Counted as paid hours worked | 29 CFR 785.18 |
| Minor meal break (14-15) | Documented 30 minutes after 5 consecutive hours | Alabama Child Labor Law (labor.alabama.gov) |
| Minor work-week limit (14-15 in session) | 18 hours; 40 hours when school not in session | Alabama Department of Labor |
| Child labor civil penalty | $300 to $5,000 per violation | Alabama Department of Labor |
| Tipped minimum wage | $2.13/hr federal; $7.25 total with tips | dol.gov — FLSA Section 3(m) |
| Engaged to wait | Compensable working time | DOL Fact Sheet #22 |
| Waiting to be engaged | Not compensable | DOL Fact Sheet #22 |
| Overtime tax exclusion | Expired 30 June 2025; replaced by $1,000 individual deduction for tax years 2026-2028 (Act 2026-604) | revenue.alabama.gov |

## Editorial notes

- Open with the punch: in Alabama, federal law does the work. That's a feature for payroll teams; it's a hidden gotcha for multi-state employers used to California-grade obligations.
