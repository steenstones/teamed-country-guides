## Answer-First headline data points (use throughout)
- Alabama is one of the few US states that has **no general public-policy exception** to at-will employment. *Source: Howard v. Wolff Broadcasting Corp. (Ala. 1992).*
- Alabama does **not** recognise the **implied covenant of good faith** as a basis for a tort claim (the duty exists; breach is contract only, no bad-faith damages). *Source: Am. Cast Iron Pipe Co. v. Williams, 591 So. 2d 854 (Ala. 1991).*
- Alabama **does** recognise the **implied-contract exception** via employee handbooks — but only when language is specific, communicated, and not disclaimed. *Source: Hoffman-La Roche v. Campbell, 512 So. 2d 725 (Ala. 1987).*
- Statutory carveouts (the only "you can't fire someone for X" rules at state level):
  - Workers' compensation retaliation — Ala. Code § 25-5-11.1
  - Public-employee whistleblowing — Ala. Code § 36-25-24
  - (Plus child abuse reporter protection — Ala. Code § 26-14-3)
- **No state final paycheck statute** — federal FLSA timing applies (next regular payday). *Source: Alabama Retail Association; Workforcehub.*
- Federal claim layer (where the lawsuit risk actually sits):
  - Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 — race, colour, religion, sex (incl. pregnancy + LGBTQ post-Bostock), national origin (15+ employees)
  - ADA — disability (15+ employees)
  - ADEA — age 40+ (20+ employees)
  - USERRA — military service (1+ employees)
  - FMLA — interference / retaliation for protected leave (50+ employees within 75 miles)
  - EEOC charge deadline: 180 days (no state deferral agency in Alabama, so it stays at 180, not 300)
  - FLSA retaliation (any wage-and-hour complaint)
- **Federal WARN Act applies**: 100+ FT employees, 60 days notice before mass layoff / plant closing (50+ at single site if ≥33% of workforce, or 500+ regardless).
- Alabama state mass-separation notice — separate state requirement: notify Alabama Unemployment Compensation Call Center Operations no later than the actual mass separation date (per Brightmine; Code of Alabama).
