## The story

Alabama has **no state-mandated paid family leave** and **no state-mandated paid sick leave**. There is no Alabama equivalent of California's PFL, no Alabama equivalent of New York's PFL, no Alabama mini-FMLA. The only federal floor is the **federal Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA, 29 U.S.C. § 2601 et seq.)**, which provides **12 weeks of UNPAID, job-protected leave** for employers with 50+ employees within a 75-mile radius.

For employers with under 50 employees, even federal FMLA doesn't apply. The leave package is entirely voluntary, designed by the employer.

This page is **honest about the absence**. It does not invent state leave laws. The buyer story:

1. Alabama is attractive for cost-conscious employers because there is no state PFL tax, no state PSL administrative burden, no SDI premium.
2. The trade-off is that competitive talent (especially knowledge workers in Birmingham and Huntsville) increasingly expects parental leave, sick days, and bereavement built into the offer letter — they're comparing against jobs at companies with California, NY, or DC employees who get state benefits.
3. Federal FMLA is the floor. If the employer crosses the 50-employee threshold (counting all US employees, not just Alabama ones), the FMLA obligation kicks in.
4. The employer designs the voluntary policy. Teamed brings benchmark data on what competitive Alabama employers offer (typically 8-12 weeks paid parental leave for retention-critical roles, 5-10 paid sick days, 3-5 bereavement days).

## Factual uncertainty notes

- **FMLA 50-employee threshold counts all US employees, not just Alabama ones.** This matters for multi-state employers — an employer with 30 Alabama employees and 25 Texas employees IS FMLA-covered for the Alabama hires. Drafted prose makes this explicit.
- **The 75-mile-radius rule** for FMLA-covered worksites means a fully remote employer might not be FMLA-covered for an Alabama hire even at 50+ headcount if their employees are geographically dispersed. The DOL counts employees who report to or get assignments from a single worksite within 75 miles. This is a nuance the page should mention without over-stating; drafted prose treats it as a parenthetical.
- **No conflict with WebSearch** — the compliance cache confirms Alabama has no state PFL/PSL, and every reputable source (NCSL, DOL, A Better Balance) agrees. No factual uncertainty flag.
