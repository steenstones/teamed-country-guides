## Answer-First headline data points

### The exceptions (citation gold)
- **Implied-contract exception**: *Sterling Drug, Inc. v. Oxford*, 294 Ark. 239, 743 S.W.2d 380 (1988). Handbook that "expressly states the employee may not be discharged except for cause" creates an enforceable implied contract. Narrowed by *Crain Industries v. Cass*, *Gladden v. Arkansas Children's Hospital*, *Smith v. American Greetings* (804 S.W.2d 683, 1991), *Cisco v. King*. Disclaimer in offer letter + handbook + signed acknowledgement defeats.
- **Public-policy exception**: same Sterling Drug case recognised wrongful discharge in violation of well-established public policy. Recognised categories: refusal to violate criminal statute, exercising statutory right, complying with statutory duty, whistleblowing on violation of federal/state law (*Jarrett v. ERC Properties*).
- **Implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing — TORT**: NOT recognised as separate tort in Arkansas. Lives in contract only. Materially narrower than Alaska (*Mitford v. de Lasala* 1983).

### Statutes
- **Final paycheck — discharge**: Ark. Code Ann. § 11-4-405 — **within 7 days of discharge IF employee demands payment**, else next regular payday. Penalty: **doubling** of unpaid wages if employer fails to pay within 7 days of next regular payday after demand.
- **Workers' comp retaliation**: Ark. Code Ann. § 11-9-107 — cannot fire for filing claim. Fine up to $10,000; Class D felony.
- **Arkansas Civil Rights Act (ACRA)**: Ark. Code Ann. § 16-123-101 et seq. — 9-employee threshold, 1-year statute of limitations, private right of action only (no state enforcement agency).
- **Public-employee whistleblower**: Ark. Code Ann. § 21-1-601 et seq.
- **Jury duty protection**: Ark. Code Ann. § 16-31-106.

### Discrimination filing window
- **Arkansas is NOT an EEOC deferral state** — 180-day federal charge window (not 300 like Arizona/Alaska). ACRA has no state enforcement agency, so EEOC defers to nobody.
- 8th Circuit hears appeals; historically employer-favourable on summary judgment.
- ACRA 1-year statute of limitations starts independently.

### Federal floor (always applies)
- Title VII (15+), ADA (15+), ADEA (20+), USERRA (1+), FMLA (50+ within 75 miles), FLSA retaliation, NLRA § 7.

### Mass layoff
- **Federal WARN Act only** — 29 U.S.C. § 2102, 100+ FT employees, 60 days advance notice. Arkansas has **no state mini-WARN**. Notice goes to DWS Rapid Response Unit (Cody Waits, AR Workforce Connections, PO Box 2981, Little Rock, AR 72203) + chief elected local official.

## Conflicting / uncertain data
**One real conflict** — the page-specific guidance said "Implied covenant of good faith NOT recognised". ALFA International compendium says it IS recognised ("under limited circumstances") via *Smith v. American Greetings*. Reading the case directly: Smith narrowed both the implied-contract claim (handbook language insufficient) AND the tort-of-outrage; it did NOT create an independent good-faith TORT. The page-specific guidance is correct on the operative point — Arkansas does not give plaintiffs a separate tort claim with punitive damages for good-faith breach. The covenant exists in contract under Sterling, but does not generate independent tort exposure. Page reflects this nuance carefully.
