## Page thesis

Arkansas voters lifted the state floor above the federal default. The 2018 ballot measure (Initiated Act 5) drove the rate to $11.00 per hour from 1 January 2021 and froze it there — no CPI escalator, no automatic January raise. That single fact separates Arkansas from Alabama (federal-only, $7.25) and from Arizona (indexed, $14.70). Overtime defers to federal FLSA (1.5x over 40 hours per week, no daily trigger). Adult meal and rest breaks are not required. The Arkansas Wage Act sets the pay-frequency rule (at least semi-monthly under § 11-4-401) and the final-pay rule (within 7 days of demand on discharge, doubling penalty under § 11-4-405).

## Editorial notes

- Open with the differentiator: Arkansas voters lifted the floor above federal. That's the headline number ($11) for a payroll team coming from Alabama (federal $7.25) and the contrast with Arizona (CPI-indexed $14.70).
- Don't oversell the $11. It's been frozen at $11 since 1 January 2021. No CPI escalator. The voter-approved schedule ended in 2021 and the state legislature has not raised the rate. Inflation has eaten into the real value.
- Coverage matters: the state Act applies only to employers with **4+ employees**. Tiny employers default to federal $7.25 under the FLSA carve-outs — explain this clearly so an employer doesn't undershoot.
- Overtime mirrors FLSA. Arkansas Code § 11-4-211 incorporates the federal 40-hour rule by reference rather than setting a separate state regime. No daily overtime trigger.
- The child-labour section is unique to Arkansas in this US-state child set because of the 2023 Youth Hiring Act change. Surface the no-work-permit fact for 14-15 year olds with the employer takeaway: federal 29 CFR Part 570 still constrains hazardous occupations and time limits — Arkansas's permit removal doesn't change federal child labour limits.

## Conflicts / uncertainties

- Coverage threshold: state Act applies to **4+ employees**; federal FLSA applies to enterprise threshold ($500k gross receipts or interstate commerce). Some sources phrase this differently. Used the verified Arkansas Department of Labor framing.
- 2018 Initiated Act 5 schedule: $9.25 (2019) → $10.00 (2020) → $11.00 (2021, held). Sources concur. No annual indexation built in.
- Child-labour hours for 14-15 (school in session): one source quotes "no more than 8 hrs/day, 48 hrs/wk", other sources quote slightly different cuts; used the AR Department of Labor and Licensing canonical text.
- Tip credit math: state min $11.00 minus tipped cash $2.63 = $8.37 tip credit. Calculation verified against Nolo and minimum-wage.org.
