Maryland has two paid leave laws on different timelines. The Healthy Working Families Act is live now: 1 hour of paid sick leave per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours a year, for employers with 15 or more employees. The FAMLI paid family leave programme starts employer contributions on 1st January 2027 and pays benefits from 3rd January 2028 at up to 90% of average weekly wages, capped at $1,000/week. The FAMLI contribution rate is 0.9% of covered wages, split 50/50. Both laws stack on top of federal FMLA.

What is Maryland FAMLI and when does it start?

Maryland FAMLI is a state-run paid leave programme. Employer contributions begin 1st January 2027; employees can claim benefits from 3rd January 2028.

The Time to Care Act of 2022 created Maryland's Family and Medical Leave Insurance programme. After two rounds of legislative delay, the programme reached final regulations on 30th March 2026 (COMAR 09.42) and is now on track. Payroll deductions and employer contributions start on 1st January 2027. Benefits open for claims no later than 3rd January 2028.

Eligible employees get up to 12 weeks of paid leave per qualifying reason, with a possible additional 12 weeks for a separate qualifying event in the same application year, allowing up to 24 weeks total. Wage replacement runs at 90% of average weekly wages for earnings at or below 65% of the Maryland State Average Weekly Wage, and 50% above that threshold. Benefits are capped at $1,000 per week (subject to inflation adjustment).

Qualifying reasons include bonding with a new child (birth, adoption, or placement through a child welfare agency), a serious personal health condition, caring for a family member with a serious health condition, and qualifying military exigency.

How much does Maryland FAMLI cost employers?

The total FAMLI contribution rate is 0.9% of covered wages up to the Social Security wage cap, split equally: 0.45% employer, 0.45% employee.

Contributions apply to wages paid from 1st January 2027 through 31st December 2027. The state announces each subsequent year's rate every November. The statutory ceiling is 1.2% total; the rate cannot exceed that cap regardless of programme costs.

How much sick leave must Maryland employers provide right now?

Employers with 15 or more employees must provide paid earned sick and safe leave at 1 hour per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours a year. Employees can't use leave until the 107th day of employment.

The Healthy Working Families Act has been in effect since February 2018. Leave accrues from day one at 1 hour per 30 hours worked, capped at 40 hours per year. Unused leave carries over up to 40 hours at year end (total accrued balance cannot exceed 64 hours). Employers who front-load the full 40 hours at the start of the year may eliminate carryover tracking entirely.

The 106-day waiting period catches most US hiring managers off guard. Employees cannot use their accrued balance until after the first 106 calendar days of employment. It's not a cap on accrual; it's a gate on use. Your offer letter and onboarding documentation need to reflect this precisely.

How do Maryland's paid leave laws interact with federal FMLA?

Maryland FAMLI and HWFA leave run concurrently with federal FMLA for qualifying events. You're running three separate compliance tracks, not a single combined entitlement.

Federal FMLA applies to employers with 50 or more employees and provides up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave. Maryland FAMLI provides the wage-replacement layer once benefits open in January 2028. The two programmes run concurrently for qualifying events; the employee doesn't get 12 FMLA weeks plus 12 FAMLI weeks sequentially.

HWFA sick leave is a separate pot. Employees use HWFA leave for shorter illness or care events; FMLA governs the extended job protection for serious conditions. HWFA leave and FMLA leave may run concurrently when the qualifying reason overlaps.

Frequently asked questions about Maryland paid leave

Does Maryland have paid family leave in 2026?

Not yet. Maryland's FAMLI programme starts employer contributions on 1st January 2027 and opens benefit claims from 3rd January 2028. The programme provides up to 12 weeks of paid leave at up to 90% of average weekly wages, capped at $1,000 per week.

How much sick leave do Maryland employers have to provide?

Employers with 15 or more employees must provide paid earned sick and safe leave at 1 hour per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours per year. Employees can't use accrued leave until after 106 calendar days of employment.

What is the Maryland FAMLI contribution rate?

The total contribution rate is 0.9% of covered wages up to the Social Security wage base, split equally at 0.45% employer and 0.45% employee. Employers with fewer than 15 employees pay only the employee half. Contributions begin 1st January 2027.

Can Maryland employees use HWFA sick leave immediately?

No. Employees must complete 106 calendar days of employment before using accrued Healthy Working Families Act leave. Leave accrues from day one, but use is restricted during the first 106 days.
