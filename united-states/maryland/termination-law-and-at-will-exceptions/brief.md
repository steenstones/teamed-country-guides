## Compliance data — sourced and verified

### At-will baseline
- Maryland is an at-will employment state (MD DOL, dllr.state.md.us)
- Employer may terminate for any reason or no reason without notice, absent a contract or exception
- Source: https://www.dllr.state.md.us/labor/wagepay/wpatwill.shtml

### Statutory exceptions to at-will
1. **Discrimination** — Md. Code Ann., State Gov't § 20-601 et seq. (Maryland Fair Employment Practices Act / FEPA): race, color, sex, age (18+), religion, national origin, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, genetic information. Applies to employers with 15+ employees (harassment: 1+ employee).
2. **Workers' compensation retaliation** — termination for filing a workers' comp claim is unlawful
3. **Wage/overtime assertion** — termination for asserting minimum wage or overtime rights is unlawful
4. **Jury duty / military service** — protected absences
5. **Public policy exception (common law)** — Adler v. American Standard Corp., 291 Md. 31 (1981): employee may sue for wrongful discharge if termination violates "a clear mandate of public policy" — e.g. refusing to commit a crime, filing a workers' comp claim
6. **Whistleblower/retaliation** — reporting discrimination, safety violations, FMLA requests, wage complaints

### Notice periods
- **No statutory notice requirement** for individual terminations in Maryland
- At-will means zero notice required by default
- Notice obligations only arise from: individual employment contracts, collective bargaining agreements, or written company policy

### Final paycheck
- Due on the **next scheduled pay date** after separation (same as if employment had continued)
- Includes all wages, overtime, and determinable commissions
- Vacation/PTO payout: required only if employer's written policy grants it; forfeiture clauses are valid if disclosed at hire
- Sick leave (HWFA): NOT required to be paid out on termination; reinstated if rehired within 37 weeks
- Penalty for non-payment: employer liable for 3× unpaid wages + attorney fees
- Source: https://www.dllr.state.md.us/labor/wagepay/wppayonterm.shtml

### Severance
- **Not required by Maryland law** (Md. Code, Lab. & Empl. § 3-505 referenced but establishes no mandate)
- Severance obligations arise only from: employment contract, written policy, or established pattern creating reasonable expectation
- Source: https://labor.maryland.gov/labor/wagepay/wpsevpay.shtml

### Mass layoff / WARN
- **Maryland Economic Stabilization Act (ESA)** — effective 13th October 2025:
  - Applies to employers with 50+ employees
  - Trigger: reduction affecting ≥25% of workforce OR ≥15 workers (whichever greater) over 3 months
  - Notice: 60 days to MD Labor Dislocation Services Unit, employee reps, affected employees, and chief local elected official
- **Federal WARN Act** (29 U.S.C. § 2101 et seq.):
  - Applies to employers with 100+ employees
  - Trigger: 50+ workers OR ≥33% of active workforce
  - Notice: 60 days
- **General dislocation form**: employers with advance notice of any layoff of 25+ workers must file dislocation event form 48 hours before layoff begins
- Source: https://labor.maryland.gov/employment/warn.shtml

### Protected leave retaliation (termination risk)
- Maryland Healthy Working Families Act (HWFA) — Md. Code Ann., Lab. & Empl. § 3-1301 et seq.: 15+ employees = paid sick/safe leave; <15 = unpaid. Terminating an employee for using HWFA leave is retaliation.
- FMLA: federal, 50+ employees, 12 weeks unpaid — retaliation claim if terminated for FMLA use
