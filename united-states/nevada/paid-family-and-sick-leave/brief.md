## The story

Nevada is the unusual US state that mandates **paid leave for any reason** at 50+ employees — not just sick or caregiving use. SB 312 (NRS 608.0197), effective 1st January 2020, requires private employers with 50 or more Nevada employees in business for more than 2 years to let workers accrue paid leave at **0.01923 hours per hour worked** (~40 hours/year for a full-time employee). No documentation required. No reason required. Any use permitted after 90 days.

Nevada does **not** have a state paid family and medical leave (PFML) programme. There is no state payroll deduction, no state benefit portal, no state-funded wage replacement for parental or medical leave. The 40-hour SB 312 bank is the entire state mandate above the federal floor.

Federal FMLA applies at 50+ employees — the same threshold as SB 312 — providing 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave for qualifying reasons. The two laws stack: SB 312 paid hours run concurrently with FMLA leave when qualifying.

2026 update: **AB 305** (effective 1st January 2026) caps the fee a healthcare provider may charge a Nevada employee for completing a federal FMLA medical certification form at **$30**.
