## Page intent

Answers the query "what is Nevada's worker classification test" for HR and finance buyers at companies engaging Nevada contractors or considering Nevada hires. The key insight Nevada-specific compliance content must land: Nevada runs three parallel tests (NRS 608.0155 for wage/hour, NRS 612.085 ESD test for UI, IRS 20-factor for federal tax), and a contractor can pass one while failing another. The NRS 608.0155 Part 2 location branch is what separates Nevada from California's ABC test — a structural distinction most guides miss.

---

## Classification framework

**NRS 608.0155 — three-part conjunctive test (wage and hour)**
1. Free from control and direction (by contract and in fact)
2. Service outside usual course of business OR performed outside all hirer's places of business (either/or — Nevada's key differentiator)
3. Customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or profession

**Limitation:** NRS 608.0155 does NOT apply to minimum wage claims under Nevada's Constitutional Minimum Wage Amendment — those use the federal economic realities test.

**NRS 612.085 — ESD six-factor test (UI)**  
Adds factors 4–6 beyond the NRS 608.0155 criteria: right to terminate without liability, expenses incurred not reimbursed, investment in own tools/equipment/facilities.

**IRS 20-factor common-law test (federal payroll tax)**  
Applies independently. Integration factor catches many engagements that pass NRS 608.0155 via the location branch.

**NRS 624 (construction)**  
Unlicensed construction workers cannot be lawful contractors regardless of NRS 608.0155 outcome.

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| First misclassified worker | $2,500 per worker |
| Subsequent misclassified workers | $5,000 per worker |

Back wages, UI contributions, workers' comp gap, federal payroll tax liability stack on top.

**Flag:** SB 224 (2023) final enrolled form should be verified against enacted language. The $2,500/$5,000 per-worker framework predates SB 224; SB 224 added enhancement provisions. Sourced from Nevada Labor Commissioner guidance and secondary legal commentary.

## Competitive differentiation vs California

Nevada's NRS 608.0155 Part 2 is an either/or: outside usual course of business OR performed off hirer's premises. California's ABC Prong B is outside usual course of business only — no location alternative. A remote contractor role that automatically fails California's AB5 (Prong B) may be lawfully classified as a contractor in Nevada if working off hirer's premises and Parts 1 and 3 hold.
