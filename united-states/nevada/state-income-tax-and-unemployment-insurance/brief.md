## Core editorial frame

Nevada is the rare US state with a constitutional prohibition on personal income tax. For employers, this eliminates the withholding complexity that dominates payroll in California, New York, and most other states. The page leads with this — no state income tax — then explains the two actual employer payroll taxes: the Modified Business Tax (MBT) and Unemployment Insurance (UI).

Key 2026 developments:
1. **MBT wage comparison resumed January 1, 2026** — Nevada Dept of Taxation now cross-references MBT returns against UI wage reports. Employers filing inconsistently across both systems face audit inquiries.
2. **Two-tier minimum wage fully eliminated** (July 1, 2024) — legacy online guidance still shows the old health-insurance differential ($12/$11). The unified $12.00/hr rate catches out-of-state employers using outdated info.
3. **SB 317 workers comp cap change** (October 1, 2026) — payroll cap for premium calculation rises from $36,000 to ~$98,400 per employee; higher-salary hires will see cost increases.
