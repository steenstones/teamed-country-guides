## Why Nevada is different

Nevada is one of only **5 US states** with a daily overtime rule (alongside Alaska, California, Colorado, and Puerto Rico). The daily trigger applies only to employees below the 1.5× minimum-wage threshold ($18.00/hr at the current $12.00 floor), not to all non-exempt workers.

Both the meal break and rest break requirements are statutory — Nevada is stricter than federal FLSA and most US states. Federal FLSA mandates neither.

No tip credit is a clean differentiator from federal-default states.
