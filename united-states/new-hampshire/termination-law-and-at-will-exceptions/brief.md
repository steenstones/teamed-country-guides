## Editorial angle

Most EOR and payroll guides treat New Hampshire as a straightforward at-will state with no income tax and low payroll complexity. The termination picture is more nuanced:

1. **RSA 354-A fires at 6 employees** — nine below the federal Title VII and ADA threshold. Small teams in New Hampshire that federal anti-discrimination law doesn't reach are already inside the state framework.
2. **RSA 275-E protects internal reports** — the Whistleblowers' Protection Act covers employees who raise concerns with HR or a supervisor, not only those who contact a government regulator. This is the most commonly missed layer.
3. **72-hour final pay window** — RSA 275:44 requires wages within 72 hours of involuntary dismissal, not on the next regular payday. Strict compared to the majority of US states.
4. **Cloutier public-policy exception (1981)** — established earlier than most state analogues; NH courts have extended the doctrine beyond the original categories.

Information gain is strong: the combination of the 6-employee RSA 354-A threshold, the internal-report scope of RSA 275-E, and the 72-hour pay rule is not covered clearly by competing EOR or payroll guides as of May 2026.
