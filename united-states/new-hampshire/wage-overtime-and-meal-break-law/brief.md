## Information-gain angle

New Hampshire is deliberately kept simple: minimum wage at the federal floor, no state OT law, one clear break statute. The information gain is in the details competitors miss:

1. **No state minimum above federal** — NH is one of ~20 states at the federal floor. Employers setting up multi-state payroll should know NH needs no state-specific rate.
2. **No daily OT** — 4×10 schedules work in NH without premium. This is a genuine operational advantage vs California-style states.
3. **Meal break exception scope** — the RSA 275:30-a "eating while working" exception is real but narrow. The NH DOL applies it only where work genuinely permits simultaneous eating, not merely tolerates it.
4. **72-hour final pay** — the single most operationally consequential rule for out-of-state employers without off-cycle payroll.
5. **Monthly pay frequency disallowed** — international employers with monthly payroll cycles must adjust for any NH employee under RSA 275:43.
