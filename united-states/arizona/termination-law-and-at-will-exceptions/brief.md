## Key Arizona-specific differentiators vs Alabama exemplar
1. **Arizona DOES have a codified public-policy exception** (Alabama largely rejects it). The four-category list in AEPA: refuse to violate statute, perform public obligation, exercise statutory right (including workers' comp), or whistleblow. AEPA narrowed the older *Wagenseller* common-law version.
2. **Arizona IS a deferral state for EEOC** — 300 days, not 180. This is a real material difference from Alabama for any plaintiff timing analysis.
3. **Arizona has a strict final-paycheck statute with treble damages** (Alabama has none — defaults to FLSA next-payday). Get final pay wrong in Arizona and you owe 3x.
4. **Arizona has no mini-WARN** — federal only (same as Alabama).
5. **Implied covenant of good faith**: Arizona Supreme Court has recognised it in employment context but AEPA narrowed remedies; not a separate tort.

## Conflicts found
None material. The four-category public-policy framing matches the statute verbatim (AZ Bar Association article + Justia + AZ legislature site converge).

## Open factual uncertainty
- ARS 23-1501 was amended several times since 1996 (most recently 2021 update flagged in Justia). Numbers cited are 2026 effective text.
- Federal claim layer (Title VII, ADA, ADEA) thresholds and 9th-Circuit-vs-11th-Circuit nuance: Arizona sits in the **9th Circuit**, not the 11th (Alabama). The 9th Circuit is, on balance, more plaintiff-friendly on summary judgment than the 11th. Worth flagging in the body.
