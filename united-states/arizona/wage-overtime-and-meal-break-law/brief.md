## Page thesis

Arizona's wage-and-hour landscape is dominated by **one thing payroll teams systematically underestimate**: the CPI-indexed state minimum wage. Proposition 206 (2016 Fair Wages and Healthy Families Act) wrote annual CPI indexation into Arizona Revised Statutes § 23-363; the rate has risen every 1 January since 2017 and reached **$14.70/hour for calendar year 2026** — nearly double the federal $7.25 floor. Two Arizona cities (Flagstaff $17.85 and Tucson $15.00 for 2026) sit above the state floor. Beyond minimum wage and tip credit ($3.00 less, with the difference recovered from tips per ARS § 23-363(C)), Arizona defers to federal FLSA on overtime (no daily overtime, no state premium rule) and has no state mandate on adult meal or rest breaks. The Arizona Wage Act (ARS § 23-350 et seq.) controls pay frequency (at least semi-monthly for most employees) and final-pay timing (next regular payday or 7 working days, whichever is earlier, on involuntary separation).

- The compliance cache lists `minimum_wage_2026_usd_hour: 14.70` and the Industrial Commission's 2024 announcement for the 2025 year confirmed $14.70 for 2025 (0% adjustment due to flat August-over-August CPI). The 2026 figure should be re-verified once the Industrial Commission publishes the official 2026 rate around 30 September 2025 — at draft time, $14.70 is the operative figure carried forward. **Flag `factual_uncertainty:minimum_wage_2026_pending_official_publication` if Industrial Commission has not yet published the official 2026 schedule by 30 September 2025.**
- Flagstaff $17.85 and Tucson $15.00 figures for 2026 are sourced from each city's announcement; verify the Tucson rate against the Tucson Minimum Wage Act Section 17-3 effective 1 January 2026.
- Tip credit phrasing: "up to $3.00 below state minimum if combined wage + tips reach state minimum" is the canonical reading of ARS § 23-363(C). Some non-statutory sources describe it as "tipped wage = $11.70" which is the same math but phrased differently. Use the canonical employer-obligation framing.
- The $684/week FLSA salary basis is current as of the 2024 reinstatement; the 2024 increase was vacated by Texas court in late 2024. Using $684 as the operative threshold.
