## The story

Arizona sits between Alabama (no statute) and Alaska (Ballot Measure 1, 2025) on the paid-leave spectrum. Arizona's lever was pulled almost a decade ago and is therefore the most-forgotten of the three.

In November 2016, Arizona voters passed **Proposition 206** (the Fair Wages and Healthy Families Act). One half of the proposition raised the minimum wage and CPI-indexed it from that point onward; the other half created the **Arizona Earned Paid Sick Time Act** (codified at **Arizona Revised Statutes Title 23, Chapter 2, Article 8.1, §§ 23-371 to 23-381**), in force from **1 July 2017**.

The sick-leave entitlement specifics:
- Employers with **15 or more employees**: minimum **40 hours/year** of paid sick time accrual.
- Employers with **fewer than 15 employees**: minimum **24 hours/year** of paid sick time accrual.
- Accrual rate: **1 hour earned per 30 hours worked** (identical to Alaska's later Ballot Measure 1 mechanic).
- Carry-over allowed up to the annual cap (employer may instead front-load the full annual entitlement at the start of each year and decline carry-over).
- Usable for: own illness, family member illness, public health emergency closures, and **domestic violence / sexual violence / abuse / stalking** safe leave (employee or covered family member).
- No waiting period to accrue; employee can begin using time at **90 days of employment** (or earlier if employer policy allows).
- Enforced by the **Industrial Commission of Arizona (ICA)** Labor Department, with anti-retaliation provisions and private right of action.

What is conspicuously **absent**:
- **Paid family leave**: still **NONE** at the state level. Arizona is not on the list of 9 states + DC with state PFL. There is no Arizona PFL withholding line on a payslip, no Arizona PFL claim portal, no Arizona PFL bond schedule.
- **Federal floors that still apply on top**: FMLA (29 U.S.C. § 2601), PWFA (Pregnant Workers Fairness Act, 15+ employees), USERRA (military leave), Arizona's state-level jury duty job protection (ARS 21-236).

The information-gain frame for this page: the Arizona Earned Paid Sick Time Act is the **most-underestimated** state-level employer obligation in the southwest. Many out-of-state employers expanding into Arizona have never updated their handbooks since the 2017 effective date — or worse, they assume Arizona "doesn't have anything" because they group it mentally with no-statute states like Alabama. It does. And the 1-per-30 accrual ledger has been a live obligation for nearly a decade.

## Page promise

A US employer hiring in Arizona is required by state law (since **1 July 2017**) to provide accrued paid sick time to all employees, with the cap depending on headcount (40 hours/year at 15 or more employees; 24 hours/year under 15). Paid family leave remains unmandated at the state level — federal FMLA is the only floor and applies only at 50+ employees. PWFA, USERRA, and Arizona jury-duty rules stack on top.
