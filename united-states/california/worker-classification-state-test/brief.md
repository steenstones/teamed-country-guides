## Answer-first capsule (the ".cite this" block)

California's worker classification test in 2026: the strict **ABC test**, codified by **AB 5 (Stats. 2019, ch. 296)** at **Cal. Labor Code § 2775**, presumes every worker is an **employee** unless the hiring entity proves **all three prongs A, B, and C**. The test was drawn from **Dynamex Operations West v. Superior Court (2018) 4 Cal.5th 903**, applies across the Labor Code, Unemployment Insurance Code, and IWC wage orders, and was upheld by the **Ninth Circuit in Olson v. California (2024)** with SCOTUS certiorari denied **13 January 2025**. Civil penalties under **Cal. Labor Code § 226.8** run **$5,000–$15,000 per violation**, rising to **$10,000–$25,000** for a pattern or practice, and PAGA actions under § 2698 et seq. let private plaintiffs sue on the State's behalf. Run the ABC analysis before the first invoice.

## Key facts (verified against california.json + primary sources)

- **Statute**: Cal. Lab Code §§ 2775–2787 (AB5 codification; AB2257 amendments)
- **Origin case**: Dynamex Operations West v. Superior Court (2018) 4 Cal.5th 903
- **Effective**: 1 January 2020 (AB5 signed 18 September 2019)
- **Court validation**: Ninth Circuit Olson v. California (2024); SCOTUS denied cert 13 January 2025
- **Three prongs (ALL must be met for contractor)**:
  - **A** — Free from control and direction in performing the work, both under the contract and in fact
  - **B** — Performs work **outside the usual course** of the hiring entity's business *(the killer for software firms hiring contractor developers)*
  - **C** — Customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or business of the same nature
- **Burden**: hiring entity proves all three; presumption is **employee**
- **Borello carve-out**: only by specific occupational exemption (Lab Code §§ 2776–2787) — direct salespeople, doctors, lawyers, accountants, architects, engineers, private investigators, real estate agents, hair stylists/barbers (under conditions), freelance writers (with Prop 22 specifics), bona fide B2B contracts meeting all 12 conditions, referral agencies meeting all 10 conditions
- **Prop 22 (2020 ballot, Bus. & Prof. Code § 7448 et seq.)**: app-based rideshare/delivery drivers exempt; survived legal challenges, in force 2026
- **Penalties** (Cal. Lab Code § 226.8): $5,000–$15,000 per violation; $10,000–$25,000 for pattern/practice
- **PAGA** (Cal. Lab Code § 2698 et seq.): private right of action on State's behalf; 75% to LWDA, 25% to aggrieved employees; 1-year SOL
- **Workers' comp**: Cal. Lab Code §§ 3351–3357 — overlapping test; right of control plus ABC for many industries
- **UI**: Cal. Unempl. Ins. Code § 621 (employment definition) and § 606.5 (cross-ref § 2775 ABC test)
- **Tax withholding**: via FTB, aligned with IRS common-law factors (federal layer), but ABC governs state classification overall
- **No federal-style Section 530 safe harbour in California** (unlike Alabama; see sibling page) — distinguishing California from the IRS 20-factor states is the headline differentiator
