## Distinctive California termination angles (vs Alabama/Arkansas/Arizona/Alaska)

| Dimension | Alabama / Arkansas baseline | California |
|---|---|---|
| At-will statute | Common law | **Cal Lab Code § 2922** (statutory at-will) |
| Public policy exception | Alabama: no; Arkansas: yes (narrow) | **Yes — full TORT under Tameny v. Atlantic Richfield (1980)**; punitive damages available |
| Implied contract (handbook) | Yes if clear | **Yes — Foley v. Interactive Data (1988)** — broader "totality of circumstances" test (tenure, raises, oral promises, policies) |
| Implied covenant good faith | Tort: no | **Contract only — Guz v. Bechtel (2000)** clarified: no tort damages |
| State anti-discrim | None / federal-only | **FEHA — Gov Code § 12940**, broader than Title VII (5+ employees for harassment, 5+ for discrimination after AB 9), **3-year CRD filing window** post-SB 807, **no statutory damage caps** |
| State family leave | None | **CFRA — Gov Code § 12945.2**, layered on top of FMLA |
| EEOC deferral | Non-deferral (180 days) | **300-day deferral state** (CRD has work-sharing agreement with EEOC) |
| Final paycheck | Next regular payday | **§ 201: IMMEDIATELY at involuntary termination; § 202: 72 hours for quit (or last day if 72-hr notice); § 203: waiting-time penalty up to 30 days of wages** |
| Mass layoff | Federal WARN only (100+ FT, 60 days) | **Cal-WARN Lab Code §§ 1400-1408: 75+ employees, 60 days; SB 617 effective 1 Jan 2026** adds new mandatory notice content (workforce board, CalFresh) |

## Lead with the immediate final paycheck rule + § 203 waiting-time penalty as the distinctive California risk.

This is the part that catches multi-state employers because every other state uses "next regular payday." California requires the cheque in hand at the termination meeting, plus accrued vacation/PTO (not sick leave) as a vested wage under Suastez v. Plastic Dress-Up (1982).

## Statute citations (verified primary sources)

- **Cal. Lab. Code § 201** — Wages of discharged employee, due immediately
- **Cal. Lab. Code § 202** — Wages of quitting employee, due within 72 hours
- **Cal. Lab. Code § 203** — Waiting-time penalty, up to 30 days of wages
- **Cal. Lab. Code § 227.3** — Vested vacation wages on termination
- **Cal. Lab. Code § 2922** — Statutory at-will employment baseline
- **Cal. Lab. Code §§ 1400–1408** — Cal-WARN (75-employee threshold, 60-day notice)
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 12940** — FEHA prohibition on discrimination, harassment, retaliation in termination
- **Cal. Gov. Code § 12945.2** — CFRA (California Family Rights Act)
- **Tameny v. Atlantic Richfield Co., 27 Cal.3d 167 (1980)** — public-policy TORT for wrongful discharge
- **Foley v. Interactive Data Corp., 47 Cal.3d 654 (1988)** — implied-in-fact contract from totality of circumstances
- **Guz v. Bechtel Nat'l, Inc., 24 Cal.4th 317 (2000)** — implied covenant of good faith limited to contract damages
- **Suastez v. Plastic Dress-Up Co., 31 Cal.3d 774 (1982)** — vested vacation rule (use-it-or-lose-it PTO forfeiture forbidden)
- **SB 617 (Stats. 2025, ch. 401)** — Cal-WARN content expansion effective 1 Jan 2026
- **SB 807 (Stats. 2021, ch. 278)** — extended CRD/FEHA filing window to 3 years
