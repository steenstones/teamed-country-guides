## Layout sections (Q-heading → answer-first)

1. **Income tax rate** — "What is the California state income tax rate in 2026?" → 1% to 12.3% statutory ladder, 13.3% effective top with the 1% MHSS / Behavioral Health surcharge above $1M.
2. **UI / SUTA** — "What does a California employer pay in unemployment insurance?" → 3.4% new-employer on $7,000 wage base; experience 1.5%–6.2% on Schedule F+.
3. **SDI** — "What is California State Disability Insurance and who pays it?" → Employee-paid 1.3% in 2026 (up from 1.2%), NO wage cap since 1 Jan 2024 (SB 951).
4. **ETT** — "What is the California Employment Training Tax?" → Employer-only 0.1% on first $7,000 — max $7/employee/year, only when UI reserve is positive.
5. **DE 4 + withholding mechanics** — "What is Form DE 4 and how does California withholding work?" → State-specific allowance certificate, distinct from federal W-4.
6. **Filing cadence** — "How does a California employer file payroll tax returns?" → DE 9 + DE 9C quarterly; DE 88 deposits per federal schedule when PIT crosses $350.
7. **AB5 / ABC test note** — "Does California's ABC test affect payroll obligations?" → Yes — Labor Code § 2775 codifies the strictest ABC test in the US; misclassified contractors become subject to UI, SDI, PIT withholding retroactively.
