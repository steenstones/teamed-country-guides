## Facts to cite (verified)

### PSL (Paid Sick Leave) — Cal Lab Code §§ 245-249
- **Statute**: Healthy Workplaces Healthy Families Act of 2014, amended by SB 616 (2023)
- **Minimum**: 40 hours / 5 days per year (raised from 24 hrs/3 days **effective 1 January 2024**)
- **Accrual**: 1 hour per 30 hours worked
- **Caps**: accrual cap 80 hours / 10 days; annual use cap 40 hours / 5 days
- **Carryover**: yes, but employer may cap
- **Employer threshold**: any employer with employees working 30+ days in California (no headcount floor)
- **Source**: DIR Paid Sick Leave FAQs (dir.ca.gov/dlse/paid_sick_leave.htm), verified 26 May 2026

### PFL (Paid Family Leave) — Cal Unemp Ins Code § 3300 et seq.
- **Programme**: SDI-funded wage-replacement, administered by EDD
- **Duration**: up to 8 weeks per 12-month period
- **Wage replacement**: 70-90% of wages (70% for higher earners, 90% for lower earners under SB 951 weighted formula effective 1 Jan 2025)
- **Max weekly benefit 2026**: $1,765 (up from $1,681 in 2025)
- **State Average Weekly Wage 2026**: $1,789
- **Three claim types**: bonding (new child), care (seriously ill family member), military assist
- **Earnings threshold**: $300 in SDI contributions during past 18 months
- **Job protection**: PFL itself does NOT provide job protection — that comes via CFRA, FMLA, or PDL stacked on top
- **Source**: edd.ca.gov/en/disability/paid-family-leave/, verified 26 May 2026

### SDI (funds PFL + DI) — Cal Unemp Ins Code § 984
- **Rate 2026**: 1.3% of all wages (up from 1.2% in 2025; 1.1% in 2024)
- **Wage cap**: NONE (removed 1 January 2024 under SB 951, Stats. 2022, ch. 878)
- **Who pays**: employee (employer withholds and remits via DE 88)
- **Source**: edd.ca.gov/en/payroll_taxes/rates_and_withholding/, December 2025 EDD announcement, verified 21 May 2026 in cache

### CFRA (California Family Rights Act) — Cal Gov Code § 12945.2
- **Employer threshold**: **5 or more employees** (lowered from 50 → 5 effective 1 January 2021 via SB 1383). State and local government employers of any size.
- **Employee eligibility**: 12+ months service, 1,250+ hours in preceding 12 months
- **Leave**: 12 weeks job-protected leave per 12-month period
- **Reasons**: own serious health condition, family member care, baby bonding, military exigency
- **Family definition broader than FMLA**: includes parent-in-law, grandparent, grandchild, sibling, designated person (AB 1041, 2022)
- **Health insurance**: continued at employer's normal contribution
- **Pairs with PFL**: PFL provides wage replacement; CFRA provides the job protection
- **Source**: Cal Gov Code § 12945.2 (FindLaw); CRD CFRA guidance; verified 26 May 2026

### PDL (Pregnancy Disability Leave) — Cal Gov Code § 12945
- **Employer threshold**: 5+ employees (lowered to 5 alongside CFRA expansion)
- **Leave**: up to **4 months** (approximately 17.33 weeks at standard 40-hr week) for disability caused by pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical condition
- **No eligibility minimum**: no hours-worked or tenure requirement
- **Health insurance**: employer MUST continue paying group health coverage at the same level for the duration of leave
- **Stacking**: PDL + CFRA can stack — PDL for pregnancy-related disability, then CFRA for baby bonding (so a single pregnancy can total up to 4 months PDL + 12 weeks CFRA = ~7 months of protected leave)
- **Source**: 2 Cal Code Regs § 11042; CRD pregnancy guidance; verified 26 May 2026

### Local ordinances
- **San Francisco** (SF Admin Code Ch. 12W): accrual cap **40 hrs (<10 employees) / 72 hrs (10+ employees)** — but where state cap (80 hrs) is more generous, state preempts. Effectively SF employer must allow accrual to 80 hrs.
- **Los Angeles** (LAMC § 187.04): minimum 48 hrs / year accrual, cap 72 hrs (higher floor than state's 40 hrs)
- **Oakland** (Measure FF, 2014): 9 days for 10+ employees, 5 days for <10 employees
- **Berkeley, San Diego, Emeryville**: all add their own PSL ordinances; employer complies with **most generous** of state, local, contract
- **Source**: Six Fifty Compliance, A Better Balance, Combined HCM, verified 26 May 2026
