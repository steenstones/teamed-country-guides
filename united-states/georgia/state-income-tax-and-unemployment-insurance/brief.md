## The narrative angle (page hook)

Georgia's defining payroll story in 2026 is a **flat tax on a downhill schedule**. The state switched from a graduated 5.75-percent-top bracket system to a flat tax under HB 1437 (2022), and has since accelerated the cut twice — most recently under **HB 463 (signed 11 May 2026 by Governor Kemp, retroactive to 1 January 2026)**, which dropped the flat rate from 5.19 percent to **4.99 percent** and set a new **0.125 percentage-point annual reduction schedule** targeting a long-term floor of **3.99 percent** by approximately 2034.

For payroll planners, that means:
1. Georgia is one of only a handful of US flat-tax states (joining North Carolina, Idaho, Utah, others).
2. The rate is reliably moving DOWNWARD on a published schedule.
3. Forward-year offer-letter take-home calculators should bake in the trajectory, not the static rate.
4. The trajectory is conditional (revenue trigger), so build the model with the schedule but flag the contingency.

This is the unique angle vs. Arkansas (also a recent flat-tax cutter, but cut from 4.9 to 3.7 over four years) and vs. California (graduated, opposite direction).

## Key facts to verify and use

### State income tax
- **2026 flat rate**: **4.99%** (HB 463, signed 11 May 2026, retroactive 1 January 2026)
- 2025 rate: 5.19% (HB 111)
- 2024 rate: 5.49% (HB 1437 original schedule)
- Forward schedule: 0.125 ppt annual reduction toward 3.99% floor (~2034), subject to revenue trigger
- Withholding form: **Form G-4** (allowance-based; Georgia did NOT adopt the post-2020 federal W-4 redesign)
- Default withholding if no G-4: zero allowances, single status (highest rate)
- G-4 over 14 allowances or claiming exempt: MUST be mailed to GA DOR for review

### Employer withholding cadence
- Three cadences set by lookback period total: semi-weekly (>$50K), monthly ($200-$50K), quarterly (<$200)
- Quarterly: G-7Q, due last day of month after quarter end
- Monthly: G-7M, due 15th of following month
- Semi-weekly: Wed or Fri after payroll, with G-7 monthly schedule by month-end
- Annual reconciliation: **G-1003** + W-2 + 1099-NEC copies due 31 January
- E-file via **Georgia Tax Center** (gtc.dor.ga.gov)
- Late penalty: $25 + 5% of withheld tax per month late, capped $25 + 25%

### Unemployment insurance (SUTA)
- New employer rate: **2.7%**
- Experience-rated range: **0.04% to 8.10%**
- Taxable wage base: **$9,500** (set under OCGA 34-8-49; unchanged since 2024)
- Max annual cost: $769.50 at top rate; $256.50 at new-employer rate
- Admin assessment: 0.06% under OCGA 34-8-180 (currently suspended; flag as factual_uncertainty)
- Quarterly filing: **Form DOL-4N** via Employer Portal (gdol.ga.gov)
- Registration threshold: $1,500 quarterly wages OR 1+ worker for 20 weeks in a year (OCGA 34-8-33)
- Rate notice (DOL-626): published 23 December for following calendar year

### Local payroll tax
- **None.** Georgia has zero city or county payroll tax. Unlike Alabama (Birmingham), Pennsylvania (Philadelphia, dozens of municipalities), or Ohio (RITA jurisdictions).

### Minimum wage
- Georgia state minimum: $5.15/hour (OCGA 34-4-3) — historical anomaly, preempted by federal FLSA $7.25/hour for ~all employers
- No state daily-overtime rule; FLSA 40-hour weekly OT rule applies

### Workers' comp
- Mandatory for employers with **3+ employees** (OCGA 34-9-2)
- NCCI rating bureau state — premiums set by carrier on class codes and exp mod
