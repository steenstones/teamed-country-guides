## The narrative angle (page hook)

Georgia's defining wage-and-hour story is the inverse of California's. Where California is the strictest state (four overtime triggers, 5th-hour meal break, PAGA), Georgia is the cleanest: a single weekly overtime trigger, no state meal-break mandate, no local payroll layer, no double-time, no seventh-day premium.

The hook is the contrast: a national handbook that defaults to federal floors works in Georgia but creates exposure on every California payroll. Build the matrix the other way round — federal as Georgia's baseline, layer state-specific add-ons for California, New York, Washington.

## Key facts to verify and use

### Minimum wage
- **Georgia state**: $5.15/hr (OCGA § 34-4-3, unchanged since 2001)
- **Federal FLSA**: $7.25/hr (29 U.S.C. § 206)
- **Effective floor**: $7.25 for nearly all GA employers (FLSA enterprise test $500K+ OR individual interstate-commerce coverage)
- **Tipped cash wage**: $2.13/hr (federal tip-credit floor; max credit $5.12)
- **City/county wage**: none authorised; preempted by state law (OCGA § 34-4-3.1)

### Overtime
- **Rule**: 1.5x regular rate for hours over 40 in a workweek (FLSA, 29 U.S.C. § 207)
- **No state OT statute** — Georgia defers entirely
- **No daily OT, no double-time, no 7th-day premium, no alternative workweek**
- **Statute of limitations**: 2 years standard; 3 years if wilful (29 U.S.C. § 255(a))
- **Damages**: unpaid OT + equal liquidated damages (29 U.S.C. § 216(b))

### Exempt salary floor
- **Federal**: $684/week, $35,568/year (29 CFR Part 541; restored after Nov 2024 Texas court vacated 2024 DOL rule that lifted threshold to $43,888)
- **Duties test**: executive, administrative, professional, computer, outside sales — title alone is not a defence

### Meal & rest breaks
- **No state mandate for adults** (no Georgia statute; federal FLSA silent)
- **Federal rule**: breaks under 20 min are paid time (29 CFR § 785.18); bona-fide 30-min meal can be unpaid if employee fully relieved of duty (29 CFR § 785.19); interrupted meal reverts to paid
- **PUMP Act 2022** (29 U.S.C. § 207(r)): reasonable unpaid break + private space (not a toilet) for one year post-birth, for FLSA-covered non-exempt employees
- **Minors under 16, 5+ consecutive hours**: 30-minute break required (OCGA § 39-2-2)

### Tipped wage / tip credit
- **Cash wage**: $2.13/hr (29 U.S.C. § 203(m))
- **Maximum credit**: $5.12/hr
- **Tipped employee**: customarily and regularly receives >$30/month in tips (29 U.S.C. § 203(t))
- **Written notice required** before claiming credit (29 CFR § 531.59(b)) — most common GA wage-claim trap
- **Tip pooling**: customarily tipped staff OK; back-of-house only if employer takes no tip credit; managers/supervisors prohibited at all times (29 U.S.C. § 203(m)(2)(B))
- **OT on tipped wage**: calculated on full $7.25 minimum, then credit applied → $5.76/hr cash on OT hours

### Pay frequency
- **No state-mandated frequency** — set a regular payday in handbook and follow it
- **Final pay**: next regular payday (no state penalty for late final pay, unlike California's 72-hr stack)
- **PTO payout**: not state-required; handbook policy controls
