Massachusetts employers with 25 or more covered individuals pay a combined 0.88% PFML contribution on wages up to the Social Security wage base ($176,100 in 2026) under M.G.L. c. 175M. What are the Massachusetts PFML contribution rates in 2026?

Massachusetts employers with 25 or more employees pay a minimum 0.42% employer share. The combined rate is 0.88% of covered wages, split 0.70% for medical leave and 0.18% for family leave, under M.G.L. c. 175M.

The 0.88% applies to covered wages up to the Social Security wage base of $176,100 in 2026. The split works like this: the medical leave portion is 0.70%, with the employer paying at least 0.42% and the employee paying no more than 0.28%. The family leave portion is 0.18%, entirely employee-borne: the employer owes nothing on this slice.

Rates are unchanged from 2025. The Massachusetts Department of Family and Medical Leave (DFML) confirmed the 2026 rates in its October 2025 notice. Contributions remit quarterly to the DFML. You can elect to pay more than the employer minimum, but you cannot pay less than 0.42% of the medical leave total if you have 25 or more covered individuals.

The wage base matters. On a $200,000 salary, PFML contributions cap at $176,100. The employer's maximum annual PFML cost on that hire is $176,100 × 0.42% = $739.62 per year. That number is itemised on every Teamed invoice and passes through at cost.

How much does Massachusetts PFML pay, and for how long?

Massachusetts PFML pays 64% of average weekly wages, up to a weekly cap of $1,230.39 in 2026. Medical leave runs up to 20 weeks; family leave up to 12 weeks; the combined annual maximum is 26 weeks.

The $1,230.39 weekly cap rose from $1,170.64 in 2025, adjusted annually to the state average weekly wage. An employee earning $80,000 per year earns roughly $1,538 per week. At 64% replacement, the benefit is about $985 per week, well inside the cap. An employee earning above $1,922 per week receives exactly $1,230.39 regardless of salary.

The job-protection provision is the operational risk. Massachusetts PFML guarantees reinstatement to the same or equivalent position after covered leave. Terminating an employee while on PFML, or constructively pushing them out, is a protected-leave violation. The employee must give 30 days advance notice where foreseeable; for unforeseeable leave, they notify as soon as practicable. Build the notice process into your offer letter and employment agreement.

One administration note: PFML is funded by contributions, not by the employer directly. The employee files a claim with the DFML, who pays the benefit. Your obligation is contribution accuracy and reinstatement. A real human at Teamed tracks leave coordination so claims don't fall through the administrative gap.

How does Massachusetts earned sick time work for employers?

Employees accrue 1 hour of sick time per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours per year, under M.G.L. c. 149 § 148C. Employers with 11 or more employees provide paid sick time; those with fewer than 11 provide unpaid sick time.

Accrual starts on the first day of employment, but the employee cannot use accrued sick time until 90 days have passed. A full-time employee at 40 hours per week accrues roughly 1.33 hours per week, hitting the 40-hour annual cap in about 30 weeks. Unused sick time carries over year to year, subject to the 40-hour carryover cap.

The threshold counts all employees, not full-time equivalents. If you have 10 employees and add one part-time worker, you cross the paid threshold on day one of that hire. Many out-of-state employers undercount because they track FTEs. Count heads, not hours, for the threshold test.

Sick time can be used for the employee's illness, a family member's illness, a public health emergency, or to attend medical appointments. It cannot be forfeited on separation if it would otherwise be owed under the law.

  • Withhold up to 0.46% from employee wages each pay period
  • Track sick time accrual from day 1 (use starts at day 90)

  • Guarantee reinstatement after PFML leave (same or equivalent position)

What is the difference between Massachusetts PFML and federal FMLA?

Massachusetts PFML provides paid leave funded by contributions and covers employers of any size. Federal FMLA provides unpaid, job-protected leave only for employers with 50 or more employees within 75 miles. Where both apply, they run concurrently.

Federal FMLA's 50-employee threshold is the critical difference for small and mid-size employers. A Massachusetts startup with 3 employees has no FMLA obligation, but it does owe PFML obligations from day one. That means a Massachusetts employer growing from 5 to 50 employees is subject to PFML throughout and FMLA only once it crosses the threshold.

What should employers know before hiring in Massachusetts?

Massachusetts has one of the strictest worker classification tests in the United States. The ABC test under M.G.L. c. 149 § 148B presumes every worker is an employee unless the employer satisfies all three prongs. Final pay on termination is due immediately.

The ABC test's Prong B is the sticking point: the worker must perform services outside the employer's usual course of business. A software developer writing production code for a tech company fails Prong B automatically. Misclassification triggers treble damages under the Wage Act, plus civil and criminal liability under M.G.L. c. 149 § 148B. Massachusetts Attorney General enforcement is independent and active.

Two calendar quirks: Patriots Day (third Monday in April) is a Massachusetts-only state holiday. Your offer letter should reference Massachusetts state holidays by name, not a generic "standard US holidays" list. Build Patriots Day into payroll calendars and benefit accrual calculations.

Accrued vacation is a wage under M.G.L. c. 149 § 148. You cannot have a "use it or lose it" policy that forfeits earned vacation. On separation, all accrued, unused vacation must be paid out immediately on the day of termination. Failure to pay triggers mandatory treble damages plus attorney fees.

Common employer questions about Massachusetts PFML and sick leave

The four employer questions the DFML gets most often, answered directly.

What is the Massachusetts PFML contribution rate in 2026?

The combined rate is 0.88% of covered wages (up to the Social Security wage base of $176,100) for employers with 25 or more covered individuals. The employer pays a minimum 0.42% on the medical leave portion; the employee pays up to 0.46%. Employers with fewer than 25 covered individuals pay no employer share. Rates are unchanged from 2025 per MA DFML October 2025 notice. Source: mass.gov PFML employer contribution rates.

How many weeks of paid family leave does Massachusetts provide?

Massachusetts PFML provides up to 12 weeks for family leave (bonding, caring for a family member, military qualifying exigency) and up to 20 weeks for the employee's own serious health condition. The combined annual maximum is 26 weeks. Weekly benefits are capped at $1,230.39 in 2026.

Does Massachusetts require employers to provide paid sick leave?

Yes, for employers with 11 or more employees. Under M.G.L. c. 149 § 148C, employees accrue 1 hour of paid sick time per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours per year. Employers with fewer than 11 employees must still provide sick time, but unpaid. Count all employees (not FTEs) against the threshold.

What is the difference between Massachusetts PFML and federal FMLA?

PFML is paid leave funded by employer and employee contributions and covers employers of any size. Federal FMLA is unpaid and only applies to employers with 50 or more employees within 75 miles. Where both apply, the leaves run concurrently. PFML covers more leave types (domestic partners, longer medical leave) and pays a weekly benefit up to $1,230.39 in 2026.
