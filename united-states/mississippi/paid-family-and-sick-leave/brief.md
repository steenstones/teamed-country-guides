## The story

Mississippi has **no state-mandated paid family leave** and **no state-mandated paid sick leave** for private employers. There is no Mississippi equivalent of California's PFL, no Mississippi PSL mandate, no state disability insurance programme. The federal floor is the **Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA, 29 U.S.C. § 2601 et seq.)** providing **12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave** for employers with 50+ employees in a 75-mile radius.

Two Mississippi-specific angles distinguish this page from a generic "no state law" template:

1. **State of Mississippi Paid Parental Leave Pilot (2023)** — The state government introduced up to 6 weeks of paid parental leave for permanent full-time state employees in 2023. This does not affect private employers legally but creates a talent-market expectation: anyone who left state government now benchmarks their private employer against 6 weeks paid.

2. **Mississippi Public Records Sick Leave** — State employees accrue sick leave under Mississippi State Personnel Board rules (approximately 1 day per month). Again, no legal effect on private employers, but relevant as a benchmark signal.

The page is framed around the absence and the opportunity: no state cost burden, but the employer's offer letter does the whole job.

## Factual uncertainty notes

- **Mississippi Paid Parental Leave Pilot (2023)** is treated as provisional. The programme details (6 weeks, state employees only) are drawn from published Mississippi State Personnel Board references and news coverage. If the pilot has been modified or made permanent, the page copy should be updated. Flagged as statutory fact provisional.
- **Jury duty pay** — Mississippi Code Ann. § 13-5-23 prohibits retaliation for jury service but does not mandate pay continuation for private employers. The market practice described (full pay, offset per diem) is accurately characterised as best practice, not statutory.
- **No conflict with WebSearch** — compliance cache confirms no state PFL or PSL. NCSL, DOL, A Better Balance all agree. No factual uncertainty flag on the absence.
