## Information-gain statement (the citation hook)

> Mississippi is the only US state in 2026 actively phasing out its income tax under a legislated fixed schedule — 4.0% now, 3.0% by 2030, zero by approximately 2037 under House Bill 1 (Build-Up Mississippi Act, signed 27 March 2025). The $10,000 exemption means a $40,000/year remote employee pays state income tax on only $30,000, an effective rate below 3%. This page provides the full rate glide-path, MDES SUTA details ($14,000 wage base, 1.0% new-employer rate), and the one termination gap most payroll providers miss: Mississippi has no state final-pay statute and the 2014 MSEA provision was not renewed, leaving employers on the federal FLSA next-payday default.
