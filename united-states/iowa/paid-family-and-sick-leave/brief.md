## Information-gain statement

Iowa has **no state-mandated paid family leave and no state-mandated paid sick leave** — employers operating in Iowa are governed exclusively by federal FMLA (unpaid, 50+ employees, 12 months tenure), and any paid leave policy is a voluntary employer decision. This page is one of five state paid-leave guides shipping in one batch; the others cover Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, and Maine. The Iowa-specific angle: Iowa's legislature has repeatedly declined to pass paid-leave legislation, making Iowa one of the simplest paid-leave environments in the country — but also one where the employer's offer letter is the entire policy framework. No state agency enforces a paid-leave minimum. Federal FMLA is the only floor.

Competitors (Deel, Remote, Oyster) cover Iowa paid leave with a generic "no state law, FMLA applies" line. This page adds: (1) the federal FMLA threshold of 50 employees within 75 miles explained, (2) the federal Paid Leave for All activity as of 2026 (congressional status), (3) the practical employer reality — market-rate PTO/sick policies and how to document them compliantly on an Iowa EOR payroll.

## Key facts (from iowa.json cache + federal research)

### Iowa paid leave — state-level
- **No state-mandated paid family leave** (as of 2026-05-30)
- **No state-mandated paid sick leave** (as of 2026-05-30)
- Iowa has preempted local ordinances on employment benefits generally (Iowa HF 295, 2017)
- No Iowa city or county has a paid sick leave ordinance that would survive the state preemption

### Federal FMLA (applies to Iowa employers)
- **12 weeks unpaid** job-protected leave per year
- Applies to employers with **50+ employees within a 75-mile radius**
- Employee must have **12 months of tenure** and **1,250 hours worked** in the prior year
- Qualifying reasons: serious health condition (employee or immediate family), birth/adoption/foster placement, qualifying exigency (military family)
- **Unpaid** — no statutory paid component at federal level

### Federal Paid Leave for All (congressional, 2026 status)
- The "Comprehensive Paid Leave for All Workers Act" and related bills have been introduced in Congress but are not enacted as of 2026-05-30
- No federal paid sick leave mandate exists for private employers (federal civilian employees have separate protections)
- Status: draft/committee stage — not law

### Iowa worker classification (from cache)
- Common-law test (not ABC test state) — easier to defend contractor classification
- Iowa-specific: HF 295 (2017) preempted local wages/benefits ordinances

### Iowa payroll context (from cache)
- Flat 3.8% state income tax
- UI wage base $20,400 (2026, reduced from $39,500 in 2025 via Iowa SF 607)
- New employer UI rate 1.0% (non-construction)
- No local payroll taxes (state preemption)
