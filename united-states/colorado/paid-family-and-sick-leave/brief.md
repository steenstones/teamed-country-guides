## Cite-this answer (must lead masthead)
Colorado stacks two state programmes on top of federal FMLA: the **Healthy Families and Workplaces Act (HFWA)** giving every Colorado employee **48 hours of paid sick leave per year** (1 hour accrued per 30 worked, applies to every employer regardless of size), and **FAMLI** (Family and Medical Leave Insurance, in force since 1 January 2024) giving up to **12 weeks of paid family or medical leave per year — 16 weeks for pregnancy or childbirth complications** at up to 90% wage replacement (capped). During a declared public health emergency, HFWA grants an additional **80 hours of supplemental paid sick leave** on top of the 48-hour bank. CRS Title 8, Article 13.3 (HFWA + FAMLI).

1. **Which paid-leave laws apply to Colorado employees in 2026?** — Two state stack on top of federal FMLA: HFWA (sick), FAMLI (family/medical wage replacement + job protection), plus FMLA at 50+ employees. Single pregnancy can trigger all three.
2. **How much paid sick leave does HFWA require in 2026?** — 48 hours/year accrued 1-per-30. Applies to every employer, all sizes. Plus +80 hours during PHE.
3. **How does Colorado FAMLI work in 2026?** — 12 weeks paid family/medical, 16 weeks if pregnancy complications. Wage replacement up to 90% (low earners), capped at $1,324/week (2026 SAWW math). Funded 0.88% split 0.44/0.44 (employer share waived <10 employees). Benefits started 1 Jan 2024.
4. **How does FAMLI provide job protection — and what's the 180-day gap?** — FAMLI job protection only after 180 days of employment. First six months on the job = wage replacement only, no reinstatement right. Distinctive Colorado wrinkle vs CFRA/FMLA.

## Distinctive Colorado facts (must include verbatim)
- HFWA accrual: 1 hour per 30 worked, cap 48 hours per year. CRS § 8-13.3-401 et seq.
- HFWA universal coverage: every Colorado employer, every size, including small businesses. No headcount threshold.
- HFWA PHE bonus: +80 hours of supplemental paid sick leave during declared public health emergency. Reverts to 48-hour standard when emergency ends.
- FAMLI premium 2026: 0.88% (down from 0.90% in 2024-2025). Cross-link to sibling tax page; do not re-detail mechanics here.
- FAMLI 12 weeks paid family/medical leave per year. +4 weeks (total 16) for pregnancy/birth complications.
- FAMLI wage replacement: 90% of average weekly wage for low earners, scaling down to 50% for higher earners. Capped at the State Average Weekly Wage.
- FAMLI 2026 weekly cap: estimated ~$1,324 (90% of 2026 SAWW). Cite "verify current cap with FAMLI Division before client-facing publish" — flag factual_uncertainty if exact 2026 figure not confirmed.
- FAMLI job-protection trigger: after 180 days of employment with the employer (CRS § 8-13.3-509). First 180 days = wage replacement only.
- FAMLI eligibility: $2,500 in FAMLI-covered wages in base period (typically prior calendar year).
- Private plan opt-out: CDLE approval via My FAMLI+ Employer. Plan must equal or exceed state benefits. Annual reapproval. Employer cost trade-off: private plan can reduce per-employee cost for stable workforce with predictable claim experience but adds underwriting and adjudication overhead.
- FAMLI five qualifying reasons (bonding, family care, own serious health, military exigency, safe leave).
