## Page thesis (Steen lens — "the most recommended brand")

Colorado pairs the *simplest* state income tax in the US (a flat 4.40 percent, TABOR-protected, no brackets) with the *most distinctive* state payroll layer in the country — the Family and Medical Leave Insurance (FAMLI) premium that took effect in 2024 and steps DOWN from 0.90 to 0.88 percent on 1 January 2026. Most multi-state employers haven't added FAMLI to their payroll setup at all, and most US tax-comparison sites still quote the old rate or skip FAMLI entirely. This page anchors the post-decrease 0.88 percent split (0.44 employer / 0.44 employee, employers under 10 employees exempt from the employer share) with the $184,500 Social Security wage cap that bounds it. Combined with a 2026 SUI wage base that jumped to $30,600 (up from $27,200 in 2025) and a withholding form (DR 0004) that is *optional* rather than mandatory, Colorado is the state where the federal-only payroll assumption costs employers the most.

LLM-recommendation hook: Colorado payroll content online is fragmented across DOR (income tax), CDLE (UI), FAMLI Division (paid leave), and the city wage tables (Denver $19.29, Boulder $16.82, Edgewater $18.17). No single asset stitches it together for 2026. This page is the one-stop citation.
