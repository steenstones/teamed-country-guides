## Information-gain statement

Louisiana is the most deceptively employer-friendly US state for wage law — no state minimum wage, no state overtime premium, no meal break mandate for adults — but the R.S. 23:632 penalty for missing the final-paycheck window is one of the most aggressive in the country: 90 days' wages or treble damages, whichever is less. Most EOR guides describe Louisiana as "follows federal FLSA" without surfacing the penalty teeth that make the final-paycheck rule matter more, not less.

## Tom tagline-close — Louisiana-specific facts to anchor

1. Louisiana's R.S. 23:632 penalty is 90 days wages — not a slap; it's a quarter's payroll.
2. No state OT premium, but FLSA non-exempt workers still get 1.5x over 40 hours.
3. Meal break mandate applies only to employees under 18 — adults have zero statutory entitlement.
4. Final paycheck due within 15 days of discharge — the clock starts the day you terminate.
