## Information-gain statement

> **What does this page say that no top-10 SERP result says?**
>
> Louisiana's 2026 income tax picture is a story of two recent, simultaneous tax cuts that most EOR competitor pages haven't caught up with: (1) Act 11 of the Third Extraordinary Session 2024 collapsed the old graduated brackets (top rate 4.25%) to a flat 3% from 1 January 2025, and (2) the UI wage base dropped from $7,700 (2025) to $7,000 (2026) — one of only four US states to cut its wage base. Together, a new employer in Louisiana now pays a maximum of $81.20 per employee per year in state UI (down from $89.32 in 2025), on top of a flat 3% income tax rate. The 0.09pp difference between the statutory rate (3%) and the withholding table rate (3.09%) is a compliance nuance no competitor page explains — it derives from LDR's credit-offset methodology in R-1306, not a rate discrepancy.

## Answer Capsule (40-80 words, pre-H2)

Louisiana employer payroll taxes in 2026: a flat **3%** state income tax (Act 11, Third Extraordinary Session 2024, effective 1st January 2025), withheld via Form L-4 exemption certificate at a 3.09% annualised rate per LDR R-1306. State UI (SUTA) sits on a **$7,000 wage base** at **1.16%** for new employers — maximum **$81.20 per employee per year**. No parish-level payroll tax anywhere in Louisiana.
