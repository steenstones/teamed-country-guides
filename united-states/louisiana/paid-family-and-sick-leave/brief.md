## Key facts (all from compliance cache louisiana.json + verified sources)

### What Louisiana does NOT have
- No state-mandated paid family leave (PFL)
- No state-mandated paid sick leave (PSL)
- No state paid parental leave law
- LA R.S. 23:342 (1997 state law) PROHIBITS parishes and municipalities from enacting paid leave mandates

### What Louisiana DOES have (the unique angle)
- **Louisiana R.S. 23:341** — Pregnancy Disability Leave Act (enacted 1983, predates FMLA): employers with 25+ employees must provide "a reasonable period of leave not to exceed four months" for pregnancy disability. Leave is **unpaid**, job-protected (same or comparable position on return). This is STRICTER than FMLA on employer-size threshold (25 employees vs FMLA's 50).
- **Federal FMLA overlay**: 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected, for employers with 50+ employees. Applies in Louisiana as everywhere.
- **Federal PUMP Act / PWFA 2023**: Pregnant Workers Fairness Act (effective June 2023) — reasonable accommodations for pregnancy, childbirth, related conditions. National, applies Louisiana.

### Louisiana pregnancy leave statute — key details
- Statute: Louisiana Revised Statutes Title 23, §§ 341-342 (Employment Discrimination)
- Employer threshold: 25+ employees (lower than FMLA's 50)
- Leave duration: "reasonable period, not to exceed four months"
- Nature: UNPAID, job-protected
- Position on return: same or comparable position
- Interaction with FMLA: runs concurrently when both apply; for employers with 25-49 employees, ONLY the Louisiana statute applies (not FMLA)
- Source: Louisiana Attorney General guidance + Louisiana Commission on Human Rights

### Federal FMLA in Louisiana
- Applies to employers with 50+ employees within 75 miles
- 12 weeks unpaid job-protected leave per rolling 12-month period
- Qualifying reasons: birth/adoption, serious health condition, military family leave
- Employee eligibility: 12 months employed, 1,250 hours worked in prior 12 months
- Source: DOL FMLA guidance (dol.gov/agencies/whd/fmla)

### Voluntary employer practice
- No state law compels paid leave — what employees get is entirely determined by employer policy
- Louisiana's $7,000 UI wage base (tied with federal floor, lowest in US) and 3% flat income tax make Louisiana employers among the lowest-cost in the US for payroll burden
- Some larger Louisiana employers (state government, major healthcare systems, universities) offer voluntary PFL; most private SMBs do not

### Anti-preemption note
- Louisiana Act 97 of 1997 (codified at LA R.S. 23:642): prohibits local governments from requiring employers to provide paid leave beyond state law
- This means NO city/parish can pass a mandatory paid sick leave ordinance

## Answer Capsule (40-80 words)

Louisiana has no state-mandated paid family leave and no mandatory paid sick leave — and a 1997 state law (LA R.S. 23:642) prohibits parishes from filling that gap. What Louisiana does have is a pregnancy disability leave statute (R.S. 23:341) that covers employers with 25+ employees and mandates unpaid job-protected leave up to four months — a threshold tighter than FMLA's 50-employee floor. Federal FMLA applies on top for employers at 50+.
